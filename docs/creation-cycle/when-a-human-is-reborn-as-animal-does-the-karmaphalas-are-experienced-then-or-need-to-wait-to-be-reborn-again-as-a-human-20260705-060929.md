**Original Question:**  
I have a question on animal life. We know that animals don't acquire karma phala. If a human degenerates to an animal, can his suffering expiate some of the karma phala he obtained during his human life? Or does he need to endure it only in another human life?

**Full Answer:**  
Yes, people who degenerate themselves (most do), get reborn as animals/plants/bricks and burn their samskaras through the difficulties and suffering they face in those forms. A dog who was a human is different from a dog who has come straight from 6:00 via the evolutionary ladder. When Trump dies he will become a brick-- and that brick is different from a brick that came directly from the ether-agni-vayu-jala tattvas. Those who have degenerated themselves go through all sorts of tremendous difficulties in the various forms they take, such that when they become humans again they are better than when they last got a human life. They may still degenerate themselves again, but it won't be quite as bad as the previous time. And some may become ready to live as humans in human form the next time they get the opportunity.
