Namaskar Ashok Mahodaya,

Great session - loved it. You brought many nice points and explained things in a beautiful way. I very much enjoy your teaching style and it is clear that the students are enjoying it too and learning well.

A few small points:

1. Tamoguna is dominant from 3:00 to 6:00 yes, but the domination of tamoguna begins at 2:00. So in total it is dominant from 2:00 - 6:00. Tamoguna is dominant from the time it begins to qualify ahamtattva to create citta, continuing up to 6:00.

2. Vrinda Bhagini asked whether when life emerges from stone, in the movement from 6:00 to 12:00 there is decreasing bondage, and you replied saying, "That is one way to view it. The bondage of sattvaguna is still bondage, but yes that is one way to view it." But you can be straightforward here, there is no need to hedge saying that that is one way to see it. The movement from 12:00 to 6:00 is a path of increasing bondage, whereas the movement from 6:00 to 12:00 is a path of decreasing bondage. In the path of saincara the increasing bondage is mediated first by sattvaguna, then by ragoguna, and finally by tamoguna. In the path of pratisaincara the decreasing bondage is mediated by sattvaguna. So the answer to Vrinda Bhagini's question is a straightforward "yes". From the moment a single-celled organism emerges from stone, there is a dramatic decrease in bondage. In a stone, mind and atma are totally dormant, whereas in the single-celled organism mind and atma have become active -- this is a dramatic change resulting from decrease in bondage.   

3. At 6:00 if there is lack of congenial conditions then tamoguna continues to apply pressure on ksiti-tattva, and you said to them that as a result, "the stone gets crushed". But we know that the stone is not merely crushed-- rather, there occurs visphota and the ksititattva explodes and reverts back in reverse-saincara to become jala, agni, vayu, and akasha.
