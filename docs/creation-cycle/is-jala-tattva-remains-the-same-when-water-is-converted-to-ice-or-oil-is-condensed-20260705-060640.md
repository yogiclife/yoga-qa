**Original Question:**  
In the current handout, we have started discussing about panchabhutas. I am wondering about their relationship to molecules. Am I right in understanding that they are different? For example, jala tattva does not refer to water molecules. For example, flowing things like oil and other liquids are also expressions of jala tattva. The jala tattva gets additional qualities added that yield different liquids. Similarly, water, when frozen, loses some of the jala qualities and acquires prithvi qualities to express the earth element. When heated, it acquires vayu qualities to become the air element. Is this right?

**Full Answer:**  
In this regard the English name is more instructive-- liquid factor. Anything which is liquid in its natural state is liquid factor. The various types of oils and alcohols etc which are liquid in their natural state are liquid factor. Frozen water is also considered liquid factor as it is liquid in its natural state.
