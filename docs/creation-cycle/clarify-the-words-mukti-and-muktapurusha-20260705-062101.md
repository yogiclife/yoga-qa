**Original Question:**  
Mahodaya, the use of 'mukti' and 'muktapurusha' in this handout is not clear to me. For example, the handout mentions that 'Those who do not perform sadhana for their mukti (emancipation), even though they were created for this purpose, go against the wishes of the Supreme Entity.' I thought may be the handout meant moksha by mukti. On the other hand, it says that saguna brahma is a mukta purusha. Since saguna brahma has a mind, He cannot have achieved moksha. Is the handout using the term 'mukti' in the sense of moksha or unit consciousnesses, and in the sense of mukti for sagina brahma?

**Full Answer:**  
Namaskar Mahodaya,



Your question about the meaning of the words mukti and mukta regards a series of paragraphs explaining the need for an activity called sádhaná in order for consciousness (purusa) to be liberated from bondage. Here sádhaná is explained in the following way: "Sádhaná for mukti (emancipation) means to make an earnest effort with an intense desire for liberation from the bondage of the qualifying principles of Prakrti." 



In the above explanation and in this series of paragraphs, the words mukti and mukta are being used in a general sense to mean liberation from bondage. The words are not being used to distinguish mukti from moksa nor to contrast the two in any way, but rather to describe the entire overall movement from 10:00 to 12:00. Whether at 12:00 one is achieving "mukti" vs "moksa" is not the focus of the discussion here and, as mentioned, the two are not being distinguished; rather they are being considered together as a liberation from bondage the effort to achieve which is called sádhaná. So "mukti" here simply means "liberation from bondage", and the specific "mukti" and "moksa" at 12:00 are both coming within this framework of liberation.



Similarly, the term "muktapurusa" for Saguna Brahm simply means an entity which is not in bondage. And in your sentence, "Since saguna brahma has a mind, He cannot have achieved moksha"---I would just bring us back to the bhava that Brahma is one single Entity, wearing different hats in different roles. Saguna and Nirguna Brahma are not different entities; they are one single entity.
