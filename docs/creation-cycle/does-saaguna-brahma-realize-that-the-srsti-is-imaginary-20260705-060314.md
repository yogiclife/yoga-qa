**Original Question:**  
Can you provide a clarification on saguna brahma's perception of srishti? We know that from his perspective, it has to be imagination, because it is not external to him. However, does he realize that it is imagination, or is he so engrossed in it that he takes it to be real?

**Full Answer:**  
So we just need to keep in mind that Brahma is one. Nirguna Brahma, Saguna Brahma, and Taraka Brahma are all one Brahma, wearing different hats. And that one Brahma is sarvajina as everything is within Him. There cannot be anything which He does not know. So naturally He is quite aware that the srsti is His own kalpana. Indeed, He is the one who decided to make Himself into many. In the last class you explained this point several times in the session i.e. that SB knowingly imagines the universe and that all the jiivas, being within the kalpana, perceive it as real -- and you are quite correct.
