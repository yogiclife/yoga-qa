**Original Question:**  
You seem to have used the phrase "more condensed" in a couple of places to indicate that Purusa (Consciousness) is in successive bondage within the five fundamental elements (pañcabhūta). Just remember that the phrase "more condensed" is used to describe where pure Purusa is more concentrated—meaning "dense consciousness"—where Prakrti (Operative Principle) cannot qualify Him with Her attributes (gunas). Therefore, the word "condensed" is a specific term used only in reference to pure Purusa, where consciousness is more dense, and not where it is bound by the gunas.

Regarding the 6:00 position of the cosmic cycle (Srsti Cakra) where life emerges under favorable conditions, whatever you said was mostly correct. I just want to encourage you to speak more about the greatness of this particular moment and how unique Yoga is regarding this subject. Especially this—modern science has caught absolutely nothing regarding this point of where life came from or how it began. So many brilliant people have been engaged in this effort for ages, yet to this day, they remain in the dark. However, Yoga has the answer to this. This is a very big deal.

I am giving you here what I shared with Sarvajina ji regarding this. Read it, and speaking about this in class will be very good. It is highly important for them to understand that this process occurring at the 6:00 favorable condition is a major thing, and it represents a historic, unique insight in Yoga.

**Full Answer:**  
1. Special nature of the process at 6:00

Having taught them about saincara dhara, you arrived to the crudest point of creation at 6:00, ksititattva. Both S ji and R ji were confused about what is meant by congenial conditions and just what is happening in  the transition from saincara dhara to pratisaincara dhara. That needed to be explained to them. There are two important concepts here:

a) Congenial conditions means that the atmosphere becomes suitable for life: there is the proper atmospheric pressure and temperature, and there is present the requisite oxygen, water, and nutrition for life to exist.
b) Life emerges:
* This is one of the great mysteries of the universe which remains unexplained in science. And here in this series on "The Yogic Life", the solution to this perennial mystery is being explained. So the importance of this needs to be expressed, it needs to be put in context so the students can understand and appreciate the great stature of what they are about to learn. There is a magnificence to this which is really something unprecedented. So it should be explained to them in such a way that the splendor and beauty of what they are learning is revealed.
* What do we mean when we say that life begins at 6:00 in srsti caktra? This needs to be explained clearly: Life emerges from stone. This can only occur because life in potential form exists in stone. Stone appears inert, but it is not. It is made of consciousness (purusa), which is itself alive. If the potential for life were not present in stone, then life could not have emerged from it. So the potential for life is indeed present in stone, and this is because stone is itself purusa in bound form. And with the shift from dominance of tamoguna to that of sattvaguna at 6:00, the inherent vital force or prana present in stone gets the opportunity to express itself, and it does so in the process of a jiiva actually emerging from stone. That initial jiiva is a one-celled organism.



1. Two things you have presented to your students so far as being of magnificence in Srsti Cakra:
a) The solution to the quandary of how it is that a crude universe could be made out of something suksma. If the seed of crudity is not present in consciousness, then how could this universe have been made? If butter is present in milk, then only it can be made from milk. But indeed there is no seed of crudity in consciousness, and yet the crude universe has been created. And the solution is that the entire creation is imagined in the Cosmic Mind. And it is reality. In a previous session you presented this in a beautiful way such that the students were able to appreciate that this is something amazing: an incredible solution to what was a seamingly insolvable problem.
b) You also pointed out to them in a nice way that there is an amazing beauty in the creation of matter out of mind in the movement from 3:00 to 6:00 with the stepwise creation of paincabhuta from citta.
c) There is a third one of these amazing phenomena revealed in srsti cakra, no less amazing than the first two and, perhaps to many, even more so. I refer to the origin of life itself. And you have described it to them in this session. Yet you did so in a matter-of-fact way as though it were a routine point. I would encourage you to raise the status of this phenomenon to something much much higher. No one in the world knows where life has come from. That and the origin of the universe are the two great mysteries which remain unsolved in human society: How does life begin, and how does the universe begin. So raise this point up and express it with the true magnificence which it deserves. As you have done with your beautiful presentation of how the universe exists when the seed of crudity is not present in mind and with the amazing nature of the emergence of matter from mind, accord that very greatness and beauty to this timeless explanation of how life emerges from stone. You have expressed the point in this session; next time help the students to perceive the importance and depth of this point. Stone is not inert: it is made of consciousness. So with this vision, a stone is not at all inert, and could almost itself be said to indeed be alive. Because consciousness or purusa is vital, it is full of vitality, it is the embodiment of vitality-- and a stone is made of that very purusa. So a stone contains within it the seed of life. And more proximally, a stone is made from mind. So it contains mind dormant within it. So the most amazing phenomenon occurs when life emerges from stone, something misconstrued by society to be inert. This is a truly astonishing event in the srsti cakra. In congenial conditions, life emerges from stone: and the reason it is possible is that the seed of life is there present in stone. And not only that, mind is also present in stone. So when in congenial conditions a jiiva emerges from stone, its mind also emerges from within that very stone. This is something amazing which no general citizen of the world could ever imagine. Nor even any scientist. And indeed, it is something which no one else has described: not in the world of modern science, and nor in the various schools of spiritual thought.

2. Also related with this is the need for an insightful description of what is underlying the presence of "congenial conditions". The mechanism by which a jiiva emerges from stone is based on a fundamental shift which occurs in the srsticakra at 6:00---that shift from the dominance of tamoguna to the dominance of sattvaguna. This is of critical importance; without this shift, life cannot emerge from stone. Indeed, Vrnda ji asked a question near the end of the session regarding the role of the gunas in the evolutionary changes occurring from 6:00 to 10:00. You responded saying that the gunas do play a role and that, for example, animals are very active and so there we see an important role of ragoguna.


You said that the mind of a single-celled organism is that very mind of the Bhuma Manas (Cosmic Mind). Here, it is necessary to state that the mind of a single-celled organism is a piece (or fragment) of the Cosmic Mind. It is not the entire Cosmic Mind.

There is a small point: you said that regarding the 6:00 position, no one else states clearly where life came from and how. I understand why you are adding the word 'clearly'—since scientists have long, elaborate theories regarding all of this. Even so, I would not use the word 'clearly' in this context. There is no need to qualify or limit it. The fact is, no one else knows anything at all about this. It is not that someone has stated it in an unclear or vague manner. Others are in total, pitch darkness. The Advaitavadis (Non-dualists) know nothing about this, religious dogmas (majahab) know nothing, and scientists as well are actually in complete darkness regarding this question.
