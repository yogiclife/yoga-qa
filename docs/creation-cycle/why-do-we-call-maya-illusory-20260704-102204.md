**Original Question:**  
Paragraph 10 mentions, "So prakriti created two illusory opposing concepts or ideas called maya." Why do we call these "illusory"?

**Full Answer:**  
For context it says in the karapatram, “So Prakrti created two illusory opposing concepts or ideas called Máyá. These are Avidyámáyá and Vidyámáyá.”



Your question here regards the use of the term "illusory". The definition of illusory is "Based on or producing illusion". So here in the passage, it is not that Avidyámáyá and Vidyámáya are not real; they are real but what they *produce* is illusion--and due to that illusion, mental illusion, we see the world as real and permanent. But actually the world is relative truth. And by “relative truth”, in contrast to permanent reality, we mean the dream going on in the Cosmic Mind. The entire universe is in that dream. And as we are ourselves in the dream, it seems permanent. But the universe is not permanent reality; it is real only within the schema of the dream in Cosmic Mind. And while we know that the world cannot be disregarded as mere mithya, yet the misunderstanding that we have i.e. that the world is permanent reality, this is why we call it an illusion. When that illusion is no longer there then we will see that everything is Parama Purusa.
