**Original Question:**  
How Saguna Brahma creates new things with example? Feedback from Anita Ji's class

**Full Answer:**  
Anila ji (do I have her name correct?), like students in other classes as well, is jumping the gun and wanting to extend the analogy of Rama in Prayag to how Saguna Brahma creates the universe, before it is explained in the karapatram. When the paragraph is only about Rama in Prayag imagining Chandni Chowk in Delhi, then she should stick to that only. How the analogy applies to SB will be explained in the upcoming paragraphs and you can feel free to jump in and tell her this when she starts to stray from the subject at hand in the paragraph. Students need to go line by line only and explain what is there in the paragraph which they have been asked to read. In this way the progression of logic proceeds in the clearest way. If students start trying to deduce things in advance from their side it does not help, as mostly they do not get it right and so as a result it muddies the teaching being given. 

- At the end of the session, Avinash ji raises a point that Rama in Prayag could not imagine Chandni Chowk in Delhi unless he had seen Chandni Chowk before. This is not true. Anyone can imagine any place whether they have been there before or not. Whether a person has seen a place they are imagining before or not is not even the main point regarding the human imagination. The main point is that, regardless of whether they have seen a place before, when asked to imagine a particular place a human will do so on the basis of places they have seen before. They cannot imagine anything original. If Rama has seen Chandni Chowk before, he will imagine Chandni Chowk on the basis of his memory of that place; if he has not seen Chandni Chowk before, he will imagine it on the basis of other chowks he has seen and imagine what Chandni Chowk might look like on the basis of his experience seeing other chowks or other such public places. If I ask you to close your eyes and imagine Beijing, you can do it irrespective of whether you have been there or even seen a picture of it. You will imagine what you think it may look like, a big city with lots of buildings, and crowds of Chinese people.

- And Avinash ji's point was I think perhaps to challenge the validity of the analogy going forward as a way to understand how Saguna Brahma creates the universe. Again, as mentioned above with Anila ji, you do not need to feel obligated to start explaining this in advance of their being explained in the karapatram. In reply to Avinash ji it would be sufficient to simply correct his mistake regarding Rama's need to have seen Chandni Chowk before in order to imagine it, and to say that we will learn how the analogy applies to the creation of the universe by SB, in the upcoming paragraphs. 

- Just as a point, when the subject has come up: An important difference between the human being's faculty of imagination versus that of SB is that a human can only imagine things on the basis of what they have seen in the past, combining elements of what they have seen in the past. They cannot imagine anything new. Whereas SB creates ever-new things in His faculty of imagination. And indeed so it is with everything humans do: they can never create anything new. All they can do is make combinations and permutations of the basic elements of the paincabhuta which SB has provided.
