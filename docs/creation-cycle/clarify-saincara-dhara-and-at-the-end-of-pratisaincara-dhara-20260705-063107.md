**Full Answer:**  
What I wrote here is just what was meant-- a stone does have mind and atma but they are dormant. Indeed mind and atma are present in all of the paincatattvas. But it cannot be separated into units until reaching ksititattva. When a jiiva emerges from a stone, its mind and atma are not created; they are already present in the stone in a dormant state.



The capacity to reflect Cosmic Consciousness is present in mind; the more developed the mind the greater the capacity to reflect consciousness. Hence the greatest capacity to reflect consciousness is present at the beginning phase and ending phase of srsticakra. Mind is least qualified at the beginning of saincara-dhara and at the end of pratisaincara-dhara. Whereas mind is dormant from 3:00 to 6:00 and although active it is undeveloped from 6:00 to 10:00.



This paragraph is describing the status of the Saguna Brahma phase of the Infinite Entity. Saguna Brahma is an intermediary phase of Brahma, which is to say that It is neither in bondage Itself, and yet nor is it free of bondage due to its responsibilities regarding the maintenance of the srsti. SB is not bound in the sense that it is not Itself the srsti (the srsti is in the mind of SB), and yet it cannot be said to be nirguna due to its continuous contact with the srsti within Its mind. So this intermediary state of SB is being described in this paragraph. The term "certain period" has been used-- and actually that "certain period" has no beginning or end. An effort is being made here to use language which makes it approachable for the new learner as they progress from the known to the unknown.



Yoga: In reality, from a temporal perspective, the srsti has no beginning and no end. From a spatial perspective it is very big but finite. And the srsti as a totality is singular in nature. It is not that there are multiple universes; srsti as a samasti is one.
