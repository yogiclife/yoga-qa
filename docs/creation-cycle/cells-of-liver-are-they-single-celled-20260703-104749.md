**Original Question:**  
Cells of liver – are they single celled? 

**Full Answer:**  
The cells of the liver are single cells. They are specialized cells made for functioning in the liver, called hepatocytes. They are specialized, but they are single cells each with their own mind and jiivatma, as you explained to him.
Yes it is true that the cells of the body all benefit when one is a sadhaka, they all get vibrated through sadhana, and that helps their path forward. But it is not the case as he suggested that they will all get mukti-moksa in this very life along with the main mind of the human located in ajina cakra. Whatever it may say in the Puranas, but that is not the case. They are where they are in srsti cakra. The liver cell is a single cell and its mind is not as developed as the mind of a mouse or cat. The liver cell's mind needs to go through the process of being a plant, then an animal, then a human.
