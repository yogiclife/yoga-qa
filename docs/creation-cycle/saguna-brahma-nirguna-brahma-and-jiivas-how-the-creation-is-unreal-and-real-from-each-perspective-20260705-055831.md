**Original Question:**  
Feedback to a session review on What Is This World

**Full Answer:**  
Namaskar Ashok Mahodaya,

Great session, loved it. All your presentations and explanations were lucid, highly nuanced, and right on the mark.



- So interesting to hear all the questions of Sanjiv ji at the beginning of the session regarding what mukti is, why this extra phase called mukti has been added, and how adding this extra phase benefits us? Your explanation was quite lucid and clear, yet it was difficult for him to break out of the worldview given in the Puranas wherein people go to devaloka for some time and then return to prithivi loka. I could feel the mental struggle going on within him between the stories he has been told since childhood and the rationality of the yogic world you were presenting to him. The notion that the former was a story and the latter a rational narration of reality similar to that of modern science was something ultimately novel and I think attractive to him. It seems he is slowly getting more and more interested.



- Small point at 21:00 where you asked Sanjiv ji why the statement "Brahmasatyam, Jaganmithya" is misleading? He replied that, "From the Saguna Brahma perspective, and therefore from the unit consciousness perspective, srsti is real and from the Nirguna Brahma perspective it would be potentially unreal. So from where we stand from the perspective of unit consciousness calling the jagat mithya would be misleading." You replied, "Yes Mahodaya, exactly." I know you know that Saguna Brahma sees the srsti as His imagination and therefore that Mahodaya's is the wrong answer; all I am pointing out here is that the students' replies often being brief, if we don't listen very carefully we can easily miss their mistakes. Indeed you know in quite a deep way what the correct reply is and in your own explanations throughout the class told several times how the srsti is an imagination for Cosmic Mind, and real only for human beings. So the only point here is that we just need to listen carefully to the answers students give. 



- Sanjiv ji, trying to point out a weakness in how the example of the magician's rope trick relates with the world we live in, asked about the Air India airplane crash and why only those present there experienced it or were affected by it-- not everyone in the world experienced it. You replied very well and in a detailed way to his question; just there were several rounds of back and forth with follow-up questions and answers, until he got it. I would just add that a simple way to have helped Sanjiv ji understand quickly would have been to directly show him using his own two examples, that they -- the magician and the AI crash -- are not analogous. With the magician's trick only those within the periphery of his mental shakti experienced the trick; whereas, with the Air India crash, everyone in the world whether present at the site of the crash or not, understand that the crash is real. While only those on board the airplane or in its path died, no one in the world thinks the crash was fake; all in the world know the crash happened and are devastated by what happened.   



- Near the end of the session, Krishna ji explained well about the paragraph but asked why it needed to be the case that the srsti is only made slowly. You replied well. Adding one more explanation that could be helpful: Parama Purusa has made rules of Karya-karana-bhava (KKB) which everything in the srsti has to abide by. He does not go against His own rules. Just as a stone dropped has to fall down and not up, so all the processes involved in the creation of the srsti have to abide by the laws of KKB. That is why it has to take time. Ice cannot become steam in a moment's notice: it has to first melt and become water; then only can it be heated to boiling and evaporate into steam. In the same way everything in the universe proceeds according to the rules, processes, and prakriyas of KKB. For this reason, the srsti cannot be imagine in the blink of an eye by the Cosmic Mind. Starting from 12:00 and proceeding through the entire srsticakra, everything follows and has to follow KKB -- and for this reason things take time.
