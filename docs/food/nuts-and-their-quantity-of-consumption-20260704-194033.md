**Original Question:**  
How much and how nuts should be eaten

**Full Answer:**  
And yes, I do eat some nuts as well-- organic, raw nuts. Nuts should not be roasted-- doing so heats them to an extremely high temperature and creates carcinogens (cancer-causing agents) in the nuts. That is due to the way the chemical composition of the oil in the nut is changed by the extreme heat.



Organic, raw almonds and walnuts are both excellent foods. But one should just not eat huge amounts of them. So we should not be eating fulls meals of nuts-- they contain too much oil to be the main food for a human being. But eating some raw nuts is fine-- and as always, best if they are organic.



Raw organic dried fruit is also fine. Again-- not the main item in a meal, but having some raw dried fruit is also a fine food.

Soaking dried nuts is fine. Just, as I mentioned, the nuts should be raw and not roasted. If the nuts have been roasted (which most nuts are, before they are sold), then it doesn't matter whether you soak them or not-- they are not healthy.
