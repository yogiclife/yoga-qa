**Original Question:**  
Do you have suggestions on the food items we ought to include daily to get all the needed nutrients?

**Full Answer:**  
Those who are not vegetarians are the ones who are at risk of all sorts of nutritional deficiencies. Plants have any amazing array of nutrients in them, literally thousands of health-supporting vitamins, minerals, fiber, anti-oxidants, phytochemicals. Animal products contain almost none of any of these things. So people who eat mostly junk food, processed foods, and animal products become deficient in all sorts of things as a result of which they do not have protection against cancer, cardiovascular disease, and many other conditions. Those who eat a pure, whole-food, plant-based diet will be missing only one nutrient: vitamin B12. That one will not dependably get from any food of plant origin, so I recommend taking a vegan vitamin B12 supplement. The bottom line here is that when eating a whole-food, plant-based diet, one need not be concerned about getting all the needed nutrients; those not eating this diet are the ones who need to be concerned :-).
