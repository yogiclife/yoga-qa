**Original Question:**  
 Raw coconut is zero cholesterol, but has saturated fats. Does eating raw coconut increase cholesterol as saturated fats do increase cholesterol.. ?

**Full Answer:**  
Consuming saturated fat does have an effect on increasing blood cholesterol levels. Eating large amounts of whole coconut can increase cholesterol levels because it contains coconut oil, which is high in saturated fat. People who eat large amounts of coconut have higher cholesterol than those who eat less. My guess is that you probably do not eat raw coconut in large amounts, and as such it probably does not have much effect if at all on your own levels. But you need to be aware of this. If coconut is among one's staple foods, I would not consider it to be particularly healthy, and it would be better to decrease the amount. I don't eat coconut, but I understand that it is a common food in South India.
