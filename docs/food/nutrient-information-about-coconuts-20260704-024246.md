Please see the below nutrient information about Coconuts. Upon reviewing the below it will be clear why none of the adherents of a WFPB diet include coconuts or coconut oil in their recommendations:



1. Raw coconut meat (pulp) is highly caloric and primarily composed of fat, with roughly 80–83% of its calories coming from fat (90% of which is saturated fat), 13–16% from carbohydrates (largely fiber), and 3–4% from protein. 



Raw coconut meat (pulp): Key Nutritional Breakdown (Calories)

Fat: ~80-83%

Carbohydrates: ~13-16%

Protein: ~3-4% 



Approximately 89% to 92% of the fat in coconut pulp (meat) is saturated fat. This high percentage makes it one of the most saturated plant-based fats available.



2. Coconut oil is 100% fat as are all oils, and is exceptionally high in saturated fat, with estimates ranging from approximately 82% to 92% of its total fat content. 



3. Coconut water is the only part of a coconut that can reasonably be considered for consumption. It typically derives its calories from approximately 75%–95% carbohydrates, 5%–15% protein, and 0%–10% fat. 



Coconut water, Caloric Breakdown (Average):

Carbohydrates: 75% - 95%

Protein: 5% - 15%

Fat: 0% - 10%



About 90–95% of the total fat in coconut water is saturated, though the total amount of fat is very low, typically around 0.2 to 0.5 grams per 100g serving. While most of its minimal fat content is saturated, coconut water is generally considered fat-free.
