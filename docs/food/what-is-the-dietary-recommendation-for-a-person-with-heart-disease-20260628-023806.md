**Original Question:**  
What are the lifestyle changes that need to be made for someone who rejected bypass surgery?

**Full Answer:**  
Following are my recommendations:

- I would encourage you to consider the 80-10-10 raw vegan diet. "80-10-10" represents the percentage of calories from carbohydrates, protein, and fat. This is the natural proportion found in fruits and vegetables. As such, it is really what we are designed for.

- A diet of exclusively raw fruits and vegetables is really the most powerful diet on the planet. It is what I eat myself, and what I recommend to all. Not just post-MI patients, but everyone. I do not think those who have chronic diseases or have had an event such as MI should be eating a different diet from others. Everyone in the house should eat the same food -- the best food that there is.

- Grains and beans are really not natural foods: that is why they need to be cooked. Raw fruits and vegetables is where the secret to health lies. 

- To increase weight, increase the amount of food (raw fruits and vegetables), rather than increasing the amount of fat in the diet. That would be quite an unnatural diet. One can understand how unnatural it is by considering that there are no foods which occur in nature that are 100% fat. "Oil" is not a naturally occurring food, but a highly processed and unnatural food. So rather than add oil to the diet, eat the super foods which are occuring in nature: Raw fruits and vegetables. 

- In India the food is based on grains and beans, with a tiny bit of vegetables on the side. And fruit is not even part of many people's daily diet. But if you look at the nutritional profile of vegetables and fruits, that is where the power lies. The antioxidants and phytochemicals are most concentrated there. Why do people eat grains and beans? To fill their stomach. Not because grains and beans contain beautiful constituents, but just to fill their stomach. However: it is much more powerful to get one's calories from fruits and vegetables. And that can be done, and indeed is being done by a growing number of people around the world.

- I see that in X's diet the amount of fresh raw fruits and vegetables is extremely small. For raw vegetables, he has only the extremely small amount and number of vegetables that are taken in North India in the form of "salad": beet root, cucumber, tomato, carrot. But what about palak, what about all the other leaves and vegetables available in the market? All can be eaten fresh and raw! By cooking them, the only thing that happens is that the integrity of the vegetable is destroyed, and as a result one loses so many nutrients which are heat-sensitive. One has taken a living plant and killed it. By eating it when it is alive one gets a huge number of antioxidents and other phytochemicals which are destroyed by cooking them. 

- Every day X ji should eat lots of fruit. Not just one or two pieces as a snack. Fruit should be the entire breakfast, and much of the lunch as well. Eat lots of fruit! Eight oranges, six apples, ten bananas, two mangos-- all at one meal. Fruit is extremely healthy food. 

- I would suggest removing all oils from the diet except for the Algal oil. People use oil in cooking food because they think it is needed for the taste of the food. I think you mentioned that for one year Kamal ji was not taking any added oils-- that was very good. The only oil I recommend is algae oil, which will provide the EPA and DHA which the body needs. And algal oil should be taken raw, as he has been doing. A small amount of walnuts and almonds can also be taken, as you are doing. But the percentage of daily calories from fat should be kept at 10%.

- I recommend removing all salt from the diet. Vegetables contain all the electrolytes that are needed, and in the proper amount. People add salt to their food only for the taste not for any nutritional need. And raw food is so tasty! No need for added salt; adding salt only does harm, it does not help at all.

And such studies may not be done for quite some time as no one stands to make money from such studies. Unfortunately, many or even most studies are done on the basis of profit motive. But on the basis of the nutrient profile, there is plenty of information available to suggest that a raw vegan diet is far more beneficial than a cooked one.

And are they recommending it explicitly stating that it is better than 80-10-10? I do not think so, as they have never compared the two. Most likely they merely believe that their diet is more "palatable" or "acceptable" to people. And not that it is more effective.

Rather than ratcheting up the oil and fat intake, I would recommend you try the 80-10-10 raw WFPB vegan diet. It is the most powerful diet on this earth. Adding fat to the diet is not going to help. What he needs is bolstering up the phytochemicals. Getting rid of the cooked grains and beans and replacing them with raw fruits and vegetables could be exactly what is needed to achieve the changes in X ji's coronary blood flow!
