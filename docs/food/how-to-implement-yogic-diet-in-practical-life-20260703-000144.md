**Original Question:**  
How to implement yogic diet in practical life?

We are pure vegetarians, and are not using Garlic and Onions too.
Now curd and dairy products too can surely stop eating.
But salt,how to totally avoid ...I am just wondering how to cook for the family


**Full Answer:**  
So we accept that added salt is not needed and should not be used. But how to apply this principal in daily life? We have families and need to cook for them.

The answer is the following: No human being was born wanting added salt in their food. It is completely unnatural. But when a child has been brought up in a household where salt is added to food, they gradually develop a taste for it-- just as people develop a taste for meat, alcohol, and cigarettes. The principal is the same in all these cases, no difference at all. These are all acquired tastes, which once acquired become addictive and difficult to stop using due to the habit-forming nature. This "habit-forming", addictive quality is common to all these products-- salt, meat, alcohol, cigarettes.

And the solution is also the same for all of them-- stop using them completely, and you will lose your taste for them. Not only that, you will not like them anymore and develop a distinct distaste for them.

So this is the way, Bhagini. The very first day without using salt, your family members may well ask why there is no salt in the food. And you can explain to them why-- that salt is very dangerous to their health, and should not be used. Within one week they will get used to this new, natural way of eating. They will very quickly start to love their new food, and will never look back! And their bodies will thank you for it for the rest of their lives.

The best thing is to introduce fresh, raw fruits and vegetables. These foods have so much flavor, but people in India tend to eat very little raw food. So it will be a new experience for your family members. Give them raw fruits and vegetables! They will love it.
