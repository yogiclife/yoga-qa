**Original Question:**  
So all meat and fish has zero carbohydrates. Carnivorous animals have a different metabolic design from human beings, they carnivorous after all, and we are not. So their metabolic pathways for processing fat and protein are different. 

**Full Answer:**  
Milk does have some carbohydrate, in the form of a sugar called lactose. It is present in cows milk and human breast milk as well. A large percentage of human beings become unable to digest lactose after they grow bigger. As babies, we can all digest lactose. But as we grow earlier more and more people become unable to digest it. This is called "Lactose intolerance". You may have heard of it. People get abdominal pain, bloating, gas when they take dairy products. It further illustrates that older children and adults are not designed to drink milk. Milk is for babies.
