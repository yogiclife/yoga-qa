**Original Question:**  
Is the large amount of fructose from fruits OK for diabetes?

**Full Answer:**  
So the fructose in fruit is protected by the large amount of fiber in the fruit. For this reason, fruit is not a problem for those with diabetes. The main thing here is that a person with diabetes needs to be consuming a whole-food, plant-based diet. In that setting, there will not be any problem with taking fruit as the person's metabolic status will be improving on a day-by-day and week-by-week basis, their insulin resistance will be resolving. A lot is written regarding fructose on the internet, but that is regarding the fructose in junk foods-- not the fructose in fruit. Fruit is very, very healthy-- including for those with diabetes.                                           If fructose is consumed in junk food then it is indeed a big problem, it wreaks havoc in the body. Fructose from junk food is primarily metabolized in the liver, where it rapidly converts into fat, leading to non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) and insulin resistance. But again-- this only occurs when it is consumed in junk food. This is not at all the case when one consumes fruit, due to its fiber content. The fiber slows absorption and prevents a rapid overload of fructose from hitting the liver at once.
