**Full Answer:**  
There is no such thing as "mustard oil in a healthy range". The examples you are giving are what we call "anecdotal". Saying that some people remain healthy despite taking mustard oil-- this sort of statement is not science. It is simply a few examples, and whether there is a cause-effect relation between their health and the mustard oil they consume has not been shown. There are also lots of people who smoke cigarettes but do not get lung cancer; that does not mean that cigarettes do not cause lung cancer. 

The bottom line is that mustard oil is not healthy to consume in any amount. No oil is healthy to consume. Those who do appear to be healthy, are not healthy because of the mustard oil they consume but despite it. This is an extremely important point. That some people may be able to tolerate or withstand the negative effects of the oil they consume, does not mean we should start recommending that people consume mustard oil. No more than recommending that people smoke cigarettes as there are people who smoke who do not get lung cancer. The only dependable information regarding mustard oil (or cigarettes) is that obtained in a scientific manner via epidemiological studies and controlled intervention studies.
