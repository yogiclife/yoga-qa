**Original Question:**  
(1) Is Jaggery more beneficial than artificial sugar?
(2) Is honey tamasik?
(3) Is red chilli rajsik or tamasik?


**Full Answer:**  
- As with sunflower seed oil, in which a huge number of seeds are crushed to produce one bottle of oil the consumption of which is highly unnatural, a large amount of sugar cane is crushed to produce a small amount of jaggery. If one eats sun flower seeds one gets the oil which naturally occurs in the seed. One could never eat the huge number of seeds required to consume the amount of oil one gets out of the bottle. The same principle applies with jaggery. If one wants sugar cane, then eat sugar cane. It contains all sorts of beneficial things in it in addition to sugar, and by so doing one will never get the concentrated amount of sugar present in jaggery. 
- In our approach, one should eat only whole foods. That provides that right amount of each nutrient which we need.
- Honey: It is by nature sattvika, but it is (1) simple carbohydrate, and (2) the ethics of its production need to be considered. Bees work very hard to produce it, and they do so for their own sustenance. What bee farmers do is to steal the honey from the bees, and feed them some very cheap food in its place. It is an exploitative practice. When any food is produced using exploitative practices, it changes the nature of the food itself. For example, cow milk from industrial dairy forms cannot be considered sattvika, on the basis of the concentration camp environment in which the cows are kept. That alone changes the vibration of the milk to something tamasika. Best is to keep one's diet to whole fruits, vegetables, grains, and beans.
- Green chili is sattvika, so long as it is taken in small quantities. If someone is sweating due to the amount of green chili in a dish, that is a rajasika effect. Red chili is rajasika, and if taken in large amount then it has a tamasika effect. Means, it results in crudification of mind.
