**Original Question:**  
The knowledge/facts you are stating are very inspiring and I truly believe true to each letter. I would really like to follow it. But, my concern is how do I practice it because imposing it on my family members is not possible. Another issue is what about the fact that we have to eat outside often, especially when we are visiting our extended family/friends or traveling?
Practically,  I can only reduce the intake. But, removing it altogether seems like impossible. Any helpful insight, kindly advise.
NB My husband is in the army and one of my son is in a hostel



**Full Answer:**  
I very much appreciate the challenges you face, and indeed, these are the challenges we face with any lifestyle change. In families where meat is eaten and all the relatives and friends eat meat, they have the very same problem. But that does not mean there isn't a solution. One needs to take a firm determination that this is the right path forward, and then one can bring it into practice with complete success. An important component of this is education: If one wants to get one's family to stop eating meat, one needs to serve them meatless meals and at the same time explain to them why eating meat is harmful. If children understand why eating meat is bad for them and indeed bad for the animals that suffered and died when people eat them, then these children won't eat meat even when they go to their relatives' and friends' houses. The same applies for salt: help them establish good habits at home by serving them beautiful, taste, healthful meals without salt. And at the same time explain to them why added salt is bad. All the salt they need is already in the vegetables, fruits, rice, and beans. By so doing you will be bringing up children who are themselves vanguards and leaders of a healthy life, and they will go into the world and teach the whole society.
