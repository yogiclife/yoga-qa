**Original Question:**

**Full Answer:**  
Your interest and enthusiasm to learn as much as you can about this topic is something I deeply appreciate. And the very best way to learn about it is what you are doing now-- getting direct experience by eating raw fruits and veggies! 



Reading can be helpful, there is no doubt-- but keep in mind that we need to be careful as to what we read. People write all kinds of things, and those things are inevitably shaped, informed, and biased by the belief systems of those who wrote them. For example those who claim that "human digestion evolved with cooked foods" -- what do the writers of this claim eat? This may likely be an attempt to justify the baked, fried, roasted foods they themselves love, perhaps including meat and fish. People usually set out doing research wanting to prove a particular theory they have and internally support. 



And the idea that human digestion evolved with cooked foods is quite a claim. Are they saying that the structure of the human digestive system changed to able to digest and absorb nutrients from cooked foods better than from raw foods? Cooked foods are generally constipating, almost universally so. Whereas raw fruits and vegetables digest more quickly and in a better way than any other foods on the planet. Keeping this in mind, it is difficult to imagine how one can say the human digestive system processes cooked foods better than raw foods. 



During these five days, try and eat only raw fruits and vegetables. No nuts, seeds, or dried fruits. None. Then you'll experience how beautifully the human digestive system processes raw food.



Yes, there are a few nutrients, such as lycopene in tomatoes, which are better absorbed after cooking them. But (1) most nutrients are not like that; rather, so many nutrients, as well as the structural integrity of the food itself, are destroyed by cooking; (2) the few nutrients that are more bio-available after cooking are not needed in those amounts. No one eating a full diet of raw fruits and vegetables becomes deficient in lycopene by eating their tomatoes raw. Rather, eating this way provides a much greater abundance of powerful antioxidants.



And, as I've mentioned earlier, the prana in a living fruit or vegetables is an incomparable benefit to eating it raw. All the cells are alive! That is an amazing thing, the benefits of which will be further substantiated by science in the future.  



So don't be afraid, move ahead! The experiment you are doing with raw fruits and vegetables is very powerful. And the secret is in the volume: eat plenty. Fill yourself with lots of raw fruits and vegetables. And even after doing so, your stomach and your whole body as well as your mind will remain light and energetic. And while eating raw foods, sadhana is unprecedented!

Answer again: These two articles are good examples of what I was referring to above-- researchers who have strong predisposition to a certain belief, and just want to make their case somehow. As you and I both know, much of what is published in scientific journals does not have value for this very reason. 



- In the first article, the researchers are themselves quite crude: it is apparent by what they write that they are meat eaters and subscribe to the idea that meat is better quality nutrition than plants.

- They believe in doing research on animals, mistakenly thinking the results can be applied to humans.

- Animal research is both unethical and scientifically unsound, as results garnered from animals cannot be applied to humans.

- They openly admit that they did not do their research on humans due to the ethical constraints that this involves to get the biopsies done, whereas there are no such ethical constraints if they do their research on mice. That is to say, they are free to do all sorts of unethical things if they do them on mice. 

- The ethical issues are sufficient to render this research dubious. Beyond that, results gotten from mice cannot be extrapolated to humans. This is well known and demonstrated. I you go to the PCRM website you'll get abundant evidence for this fact:s The biology and physiology of mice is very different from that of human beings and hence, extrapolation from one species to the other is scientificaly unsound. 



1. Here below the researchers state their belief that animal-based foods are easier to digest, and that cooking is an "improved" method of food processing. They subscribe to the belief that meat was an "important" food for the early humans. This is unlikely-- rather, early humans ate mostly fruits and leaves.



"Ancestral humans underwent marked increases in body size and brain volume coupled with reductions in tooth and gut size beginning approximately 2 Ma (2 million years ago). These biological features indicate the consumption of an easier-to-digest diet with increased caloric density, and have been argued to reflect a heavier reliance on animal foods and improved methods of food processing, including cooking. Cooking enhances nutrient digestibility and reduces diet-induced thermogenesis, thereby substantially increasing the energy gained from important hominin foods like meat and tubers."



2. Here the researchers acknowledge that ethically (as well as practically), doing this experiment on humans would have been very difficult: 



"Studies of genetic adaptation to a cooked diet cannot easily be performed in humans because of the rigorous experimental controls and tissue biopsies required."



3. So instead they used 24 mice, whom they fed cow's meat (beef) to and then "sacrificed" (killed) on day 5 so they could take out their liver: 



"In total, 24 adult male BALB/c mice were fed homogeneous diets of lean beef (Bos taurus) or sweet potato (Ipomoea batatas) served raw or cooked for a period of 5 days. On Day 5, mice were briefly fasted to encourage feeding on demand. Two hours into the Day 5 meal, mice were sacrificed and liver tissue was harvested and flash-frozen within 60 s of death."
