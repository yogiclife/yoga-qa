**Original Question:**  
Salt in small amount is OK?

**Full Answer:**  
You explained the point of salt really nicely. What you then went on to say, that you yourself had continued taking salt in smaller amounts, as you didn't have trust that it really needed to be completely eliminated-- my sense is that this has the effect of undermining your message that people should stop taking salt. When after that one brother asked you whether salt really needed to be eliminated completely you said that he could continue taking a small amount daily (1/4 to 1/3 tsp/day). That opens the door for people to think they can continue to use salt-- as one sister said when it was her turn to summarize the slide, i.e. that if it is difficult to eliminate completely, one can continue small amounts. And of course that is a slippery slope-- using small amounts of an addictive substance leads all-too-easily to using large amounts. It is much easier and cleaner to give the straight guideline that all S-O-S is to be eliminated. 



Guiding people that they can continue small amounts of salt also goes against the excellent strategies you provided for winning against addictive foods: making a rapid and dramatic change; totally removing the addictive item. 



In conclusion: when something is well-known to cause disease and to be addictive, then there isn't any benefit to telling them they can continue it in small amounts. Even a small amount of a bad thing cannot be good.
