**Full Answer:**  
As we know, people residing in India often mention pesticides as a reason not to eat raw fruits and vegetables. But to this our reply is: Everything they eat has pesticides in it. Do they think the rice, dal, and roti they prepare and eat daily doesn't have pesticides in it? Indeed, the rice, dal, and wheat flour they purchase at the market has pesticides in it just as the fruits and vegetables do. It is not as though by avoiding raw fruits and vegetables they are avoiding pesticides. So due to this fact of their omnipresence, the pesticides are a non-issue. Among all the food available at the market, pesticides are present-- and even more so if anyone is taking milk/eggs/meat, as the animals collect and concentrate it in their bodies. 



So here the point is that when pesticides and fertilizers are present in all the food, then the effect of these chemicals is similar in all the various foods and, on a comparative basis, should simply be ignored. If one can get organic produce, that is very good indeed. But if it is not available, then the issue of pesticides as a means for selecting one food item above others is a non-issue as it is present in all.



How then should we select our foods? On the basis of the nutritional value, the health value of each. And raw fruits and vegetables are the healthiest foods anyone can eat.
