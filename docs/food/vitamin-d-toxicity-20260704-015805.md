**Full Answer:**  
It is true that Vitamin D can be toxic in very high levels, but because of its wide therapeutic index, vitamin D toxicity is extremely rare. Toxicity does occur, but only at excessively high doses. Doses more than 50,000 IU/day raise levels to more than 150 ng/ml and are associated with hypercalcemia and hyperphosphatemia. For most people, vitamin D toxicity only occurs with vitamin D doses above 10,000 IU per day. As mentioned though, it is extremely rare. In the doses people generally take i.e. 5000 IU/day (not 10,000 and certainly not 50,000 IU), as a rule no one will develop toxicity.
