**Original Question:**  
As per the Ayurveda the vata type body should not have more raw vegetables. How can we reconcile this?

**Full Answer:**  
I find their usage of categorization of people as kappha, pitta, and vata to be over-generalized i.e. applied too much as a blanket universal rule when it cannot be done in such a blind sort of matter.

All the pathies are accepted in yoga: allopathy, ayurveda, homeopathy, acupuncture. Of all the pathies, homeopathy is the best. It uses medicine which is very subtle, and works at a very subtle level in the body. Moreover, if even it does not work, but it will not harm. And this is not true with allopathy or ayurveda.

Also: even within any particular pathy, the recommendation or opinion of one doctor may vary from another. So one should put all one's trust in one any single doctor says. 

With regard to consumption of raw vegetables, I would council people not to blindly avoid raw vegetables -- the healthiest food in the universe -- because an ayuvedic doctor told them they have vata body-type. Rather, I would recommend that the person try eating various raw vegetables -- with an open and inquisitive mind -- and genuinely see what the effect is on their body. And in order to observe the effect, one should eat the vegetable alone i.e. not at a meal where they are eating all sorts of other foods. Because raw vegetables are so very health supporting and health preserving, to remove them from the diet on the basis of such an idea would be a great mistake.
