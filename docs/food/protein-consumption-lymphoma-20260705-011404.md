One student asked about cancer and the need for "protein" consumption. Their family member has lymphoma and the doctor ordered them to consume more "protein". As we know, protein is not a food. As such, cancer patients should also eat a WFPB no SOS diet. The doctors are blind, totally in the dark regarding food and nutrition. They tell cancer patients to eat a "high protein diet" only because they see their patients getting thin and wasting away, and they think eating more protein may help. But the point is to eat a healthy diet, not a high protein diet. A truly healthy diet will strengthen their immune system as well as make them physically more strong and vigorous. And yes, as you explained, muscles grow through exercise. Just eating food doesn't make strong muscles. But for cancer patients the main point is that they should eat a healthy diet. There is no need to consume huge amounts of dal, eggs, meat. The best approach is a healthy diet -- WFPB no SOS.
