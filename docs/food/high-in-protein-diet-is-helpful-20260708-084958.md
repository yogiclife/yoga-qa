**Original Question:**  
Some study found a diet "high in protein" helpful means relative to the diet that they had been eating earlier.

**Full Answer:**  
Just remember that all these dietary approaches are relative to one another-- that is, that some study found a diet "high in protein" helpful means relative to the diet that they had been eating earlier. It does not mean that it would be helpful relative to a WFPB no SOS diet. And if the "high protein" diet contained meat and eggs as it probably did, their findings do not indicate that this diet would be good for those patients in the long term. Studies are extremely limited in their perspective and need to be understood as such. I would say that the entire concept of a "high-protein" diet represents an extremely narrow view of food-- as though protein were the only thing that mattered in a cancer patient. A cancer patient is a human being, not just a lump of cancer-- and as such will benefit from all the wonderful things that plants contain. And of course, those narrow-view studies did not look at any of those other factors. A WFPB no SOS, predominantly raw vegetable and fruit diet is the most powerful for all human beings on the planet including the entire spectrum of diseases.
