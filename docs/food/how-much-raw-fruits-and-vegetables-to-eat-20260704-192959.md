**Original Question:**  
How much to eat?

**Full Answer:**  
One will eat a larger volume of food when it is raw as it is less calorie-dense. But it does not need any sort of calculation or "counting of calories". Eating is a very natural process, and raw fruits and vegetables are the most natural food for humans. Your body will tell you at any given meal when you've eaten sufficiently. 



There is absolutely no issue with calories. No one eating a raw food diet loses weight beneath their ideal weight. Your weight will be perfect on a raw food vegan diet consisting of whole fruits and vegetables. 



That said, I am not saying that people should not cook their food if they want to. So long as the food consists of whole fruits, vegetables, grains, and beans, it is fine. So no animal products, and no added oils. This is an extremely healthy way of eating, and it is totally ethical as well. I am just saying that the very healthiest food one can eat is living fruits and vegetables. One cannot compare a vegetable which is alive with one that has been killed due to heating. It is not at all an ethical issue, I want to ensure this is clear. It is just that when the cells in the vegetable are alive, they provide all sorts of things to the body, they contain so many wonderful antioxidants and phyto-chemicals which are destroyed by heat.
