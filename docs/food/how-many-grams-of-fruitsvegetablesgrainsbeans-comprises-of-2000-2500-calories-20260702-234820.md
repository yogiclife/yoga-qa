**Original Question:**  
I don't know how many grams of fruits/vegetables/grains/beans comprises of 2000 - 2500 calories (as it is needed everyday). I don't know its amount, so please can you provide an approximate grams required to meet daily calories requirement.

**Full Answer:**  
Regarding amount of food to be taken: Fruits and vegetables are naturally much less calorie-dense than any animal product. They are also less calorie-dense than grains and beans. As a result, if one is eating mostly raw fruits and vegetables as I do, then one will be eating what appears as a much larger quantity of food. Fruits and vegetables are highly nutrient dense: they contain literally thousands of health-supporting anti-oxidants and phyto-chemicals. But these are micronutrients. That means they do not contain any calories. Macronutrients (carbohydrates, protein, fat) are what contain calories. That is the difference between macronutrients and micronutrients. So fruits and vegetables contain thousands of micronutrients, and a lot of water, and relatively few calories. As a result, one needs to eat more of them. For lunch I may eat 5 applies, 5 oranges, a large bunch of grapes, and a bunch if bananas. To people who are not used to eating this way it sounds like a lot of food. But it isn't, it is quite a normal amount. So explain to people that at a given meal or raw fruits and vegetables, they should continue eating so long as they are hungry. There is absolutely no need to count calories. Your body will tell you when you've eaten a sufficient amount. Let it be a natural process. People do not need to count calories or measure their food. They should simply eat their food, and the rest will take care of itself. Reassure your students that this is the best food and the best way to live a human life.
