**Original Question:**  
Does culture/samskriti refer to the collective or to individuals also?



The reason for my question is as follows. You had once mentioned that 99% of people are inherently good and could have led a true human life with the right guidance, while 1% are inherently bad. I assume our discussion on the expressions of human culture are with respect to the common customs of the 99%.



The behavior of these 1%, who take pleasure in harming people, would not be considered valid expressions of human culture, would it?



[The context is a discussion in class that mentioned that different human customs are a part of a common human culture, although some customs may be lower expressions of it, such as eating meat.]

**Full Answer:**  
Yes Mahodaya, that is correct. Those 1% (or less) are mentally ill from birth, so we would not include their behavior in the range of human culture.
