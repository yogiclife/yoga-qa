**Original Question:**  
Mahodaya, I am planning to write an advance directive. There are a few choices involving ethical issues. Can you clarify the following in light of yoga philosophy?

1. Goal in case of serious irreversible condition.

    Shall I select comfort over life prolongation? It seems to me that if the condition is serious enough that I cannot decide, then I cannot do sadhana either, which makes prolongation of life futile. 

2. Life sustaining treatments. 

    I thought I would have these only if likely to help recovery. The rationale is the same as above. 

3. Terminal or end stage condition. 

    For the same reason as above, there would be no point in prolonging life 

4. Pain management 

    This question, on pain relief even if it shortens life, is hard. What would be a reasonable answer? My pain tolerance is extremely low. 

**Full Answer:**  
These are very good questions. Indeed, quite important. Kindly see the replies below:

1. Goal in case of serious irreversible condition. Shall I select comfort over life prolongation? 

    => Yes. Here we are talking about a "serious irreversible condition", as you say, and one which is already quite advanced to the point where it is irreversible, severely disabling, and also causing significant discomfort. In that situation, the yogic approach is to let nature take its course so that the sadhaka can reach to the next stage, whether it be mukti-moksa or rebirth. 
 
     It seems to me that if the condition is serious enough that I cannot decide, then I cannot do sadhana either, which makes prolongation of life futile. 

    => Yes, agreed.

2. Life sustaining treatments.

    I thought I would have these only if likely to help recovery. The rationale is the same as above. 
 
    => Yes, agreed. And again, we are talking about an advanced, untreatable, irreversible, severely disabling condition.

3. Terminal or end stage condition.

    For the same reason as above, there would be no point in prolonging life 

    => Yes, agreed. And again, we are talking about an advanced, untreatable, irreversible, severely disabling condition.

4. Pain management 

    This question, on pain relief even if it shortens life, is hard. What would be a reasonable answer? My pain tolerance is extremely low. 

    => It is fine to use pain relief, Mahodaya. We are not talking about an ordinary situation. We are talking about a situation in which the end of life is approaching no matter what we do, and in that sort of situation, using pain medication is fine. And frankly, most pain medication will not shorten life. That would be quite unusual. Perhaps only on the last day.
