**Full Answer:**  
Organ donation 

Is this okay?

=> Yes, it is very good.  

In the unfortunate circumstances of a rebirth, would this impact our body in a future birth? 

=> No, it would not. Organ donation is done only after the mind leaves the body. Once the mind leaves the body, no connection whatsoever remains with that body.

It seems that it would depend on a mind experiencing trauma from losing an organ. I recall that you mentioned that the mind and atma are still associated with the body in those in a vegetative state. Should I say that only after the heart has also stopped should they harvest any organ? I guess the mind would have left the body after the heart failed. 

=> The brain is the adhara of the mind. The mind can only remain in the body so long as the brain is alive. So the brain is the critical component, rather than the heart. If the heart is still running but the brain has died, then the mind will have left the body. And if the brain is still alive but the heart has stopped, then the mind will still be in the body. So brain death is the pivotal point here. You can specify in your advance directive regarding organ donation after brain death (DBD) versus donation after circulatory death (DCD). The DBD criteria are such that it will not create a situation such that the mind is still in the body and getting impacted by the organ donation.
