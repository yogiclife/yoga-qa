**Original Question:**  
When Mukti & When Moksha

**Full Answer:**  
I'll give the reply here quickly: Whether one merges with Cosmic Mind (Saguna Brahma) or Shuddha Purusa (Nirguna Brahma) one has to go beyond one's small existence. It is important to be very, very clear here: merger with SB is not attained by murti puja. Your students are going to think merging with SB is attained by murti puja as the murti has gunas and so does SB. But it is not at all like that-- this needs to be clarified. One will not merge with any form of Brahma via murti puja. The murti is made of wood/clay/stone and by sitting in front of it and making one's citta take the form of that murti all that will happen is one will become one with the wood/clay/stone which your citta is taking the form of. 



To reach to either mukti or moksa requires yoga sadhana. And if at the time of death one gets mukti, that means one merges into Cosmic Mind and one's mind stuff gets recycled into the creation. And one existence as the unit being one was is finished, so one will not be reborn, and thus cannot get moksa. Whereas if at the time of death one gets moksa, that means one merges into the ocean of shuddha purusa and one's mindstuff will not get recycled into the srsti. It is permanent merger into the infinite purusa. That is the goal of human life.
