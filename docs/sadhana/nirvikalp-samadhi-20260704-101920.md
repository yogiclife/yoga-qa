**Original Question:**  
So to understand, when the mind is concentrated on Mahattattva , ( I exist ) still remains is Savikalpa samadhi. And when mind withdraws completely from Mahattattva too, to Unit consciousness, where there is no feeling of I exist, then is it Nirvikalpa Samadhi ?

**Full Answer:**  
So you have written, "when the mind is concentrated on Mahattattva"-- the mind is concentrated on Parama Purusa, not on mahattattva. The visaya of the mind is Parama Purusa in both cases-- savikalpa and nirvikalpa samadhi. In savikalpa samadhi the mind is withdrawn back to mahattattva, which becomes temporarily suspended in (i.e. one with) Cosmic Mind; in nirvikalpa samadhi the mind is withdrawn back to jiivatma, which becomes temporarily one with Nirguna Brahma.
