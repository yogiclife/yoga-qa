Clarification provided by Acharya ji

**Full Answer:**  
Ardha Ishvara Pranidhana is referred to as the process of yoga happening 24 hours a day.This process can happen while we are sleeping too.There are three points which are central in one's movement to return to the Supreme Entity. They are shravana (hearing about the Supreme), manana (ideating on the Supreme) and nididhyásana (meditating on the Supreme with an unbroken flow of mind). The offer of one's mind to the Lord is nididhyásana. The inner sense of nididhyásana is that all the propensities of the mind are to be concentrated at a point to be offered to Parama Puruśa. Nididhyásana makes it possible to merge with the Supreme Entity. It is an entire flow of life which creates a conducive environment for the mind to become deeply engaged in sadhana. And in this setting, iishvara pranidhana is one of the specific lessons of sadhana in which one carries this out.
