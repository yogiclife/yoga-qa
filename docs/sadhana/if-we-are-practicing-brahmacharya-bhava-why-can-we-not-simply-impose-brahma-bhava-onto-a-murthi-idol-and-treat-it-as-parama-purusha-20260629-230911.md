**Original Question:**  
"If we are practicing Brahmacharya Bhava, why can we not simply impose Brahma Bhava onto a Murthi (idol) and treat it as Parama Purusha?

**Full Answer:**  
So your student's question is excellent, it gives a very good opening for a deeper discussion of this important point. 

- The answer you provided regarding the idea that the ant does not have the qualities of the Pradhana Mantrii and thus cannot represent him, and similarly an idol does not have the qualities of Parama Purusa and so cannot represent Him-- this is an important part of the answer indeed.

In addition, the student is raising the question in the context of Brahmacarya Bhava, and this gives us the opportunity to explain why it is that Brahmacarya Bhava in no way justifies idol worship; rather, a proper understanding of Brahmacarya Bhava makes it all the more clear why idol worship does not make sense. 

There are two points:

1. Brahmacarya is the bhava that the object or jiiva before me is the qualified manifestation of purusa. The girl, the boy, the cloud, the tree, the soil, the river, the building, the mountain, the leaf, the old woman, the new-born baby. It is the ideation that everything I see, hear, touch, smell, taste is the qualified manifestation of purusa, consciousness. Central to this bhava is the feeling that everything is made of consciousness-- each and everything, all that I see before me. And an essential corollary (natural consequence) of the mula bhava of brahmacarya just expressed, is that, as everything is a manifestion of consciousness, there is nothing that is more made of consciousness than anything else-- every object is 100% made of consciousness, so we can never ever say that God is more present in one object than another. And if anyone ever says such a thing or acts in a way that demonstrates they believe such a thing i.e. that God is more present in object 'A' than in object 'B', then it means they do not understand Brahmacarya at all and could not up until then in their life experience Brahmacarya Bhava. Those who practice idol worship do so because they believe that God is more present in that stone than in other stones. तब न उस पत्थर के सामने खड़े होकर पूजा कर रहे हैं । और न कि दूसरे पत्थर के सामने ।  Why do they only do their worship in front of that stone and not other stones? It proves that they think there is something special about that particular stone-- and that goes 1000% against brahmacarya bhava. Everything is made of purusa in the very same way. There can never be anything special about one object over another from the spiritual perspective. All things are the same: gunanvita purusa. But murti pujakas do not believe that. If they want to do murti puja, then why don't they do it in front of the pebble in the road? Why don't they do it in front of the patch of grass? They do not believe in brahmacarya, and that is why they do idol worship, believing that there is something special about that particular stone.

2. The second point is that brahmacarya means that yes, everything is the qualified manifestation of purusa. But the fact that we feel and experience this in our every-day lives as sadhakas, doesn't mean we will start worshipping everything. That an object is gunanvita purusa does not make it "holy". Purusa and Parama Purusa are not the same thing. He works from His nucleus. The fingernail of a newborn baby is made of purusa; that doesn't mean that the baby's fingernail is the controller of the universe. The sense is that brahmacarya bhava does not make that object pujaniiya. Only that centre of the consciousness, only that controller of the universe is pujaniiya; nothing else is pujaniiya.

So the central point here is that brahmacarya bhava says that everything in the universe is made of consciousness-- and no one thing more than another. Everything is 100% made of consciousness. And at the same time, the fact that an object is 100% made of consciousness does not make it worthy of worship. Only Parama Purusa is worthy of worship, only Parama Purusa is pujaniiya. And being formless, the only way to come in the samsparsha of Parama Purusa in order to worship Him is through sadhana. Going inward and doing concentrated thinking on Him.
