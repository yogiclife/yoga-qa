**Original Question:**  
NamaskaraH mahodaya. I was wondering about the differences in the paths and final states of mukti versus moksha. I guess our decision on what we aspire for would depend on the end state primarily, but also on the difficulties of the path to achieving them. 

Am I right in understanding that the path to mukti is through worship of saguna purusha and the path to moksha is through meditation on nirguna purusha?

Is the bliss we get from puja a preview of our state when we get mukti? Will our experience during meditation give us a preview of our state when we get moksha?

**Full Answer:**  
The goal of life for a human being should be atma-moksa and jagat-hita. Really moksa is the goal rather than mukti. Mukti is not a small thing no doubt, but the goal should be moksa. Sadhana lessons are taught with this in mind.

And in reply to your second and third paras, the answer to both is yes. I'd just want to specify that we are talking about meditation on that entity, rather than worship of that entity. People may mean many things when they use the word 'worship'. So just to be clear, we are talking about sadhana or meditation.
