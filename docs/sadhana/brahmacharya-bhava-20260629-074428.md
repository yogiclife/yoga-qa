**Original Question:**  
Clarification from Acharya ji

**Full Answer:**  
I would emphasize more that the ideation is that everything is made of Brahma, not that it comes from Brahma. The child who is facing you, the dog running in the street, the insect flying in the air, each one is made of Brahma. Each is a qualified form of Brahma only. Instead of telling them keep their connection with Parama Purusa, emphasize that in brahmacarya we are to feel that everything we are interacting with is Brahma only. This is the practical approach. In brahmacarya we are doing adhyaropa that each object and each jiiva is Brahma, a qualified form of consciousness. 

- You told them many times "brahmaNi vicaraNam iti brahmacaryam". But it needs explaining the meaning. Don't assume they understand Samskrtam. No matter how many times we say "brahmaNi", if they don't understand that rupa then it is meaningless for them. So it needs explaining that "brahmaNi vicaraNam iti brahmacaryam" means to move through the world with the ideation that one is moving in Brahma. Everything they see, hear, touch, smell-- everything is Brahma.
