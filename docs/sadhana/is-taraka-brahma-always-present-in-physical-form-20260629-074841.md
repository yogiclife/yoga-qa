**Original Question:**  
The handout on _Intuitional Practice and Its Necessity_, mentions as if a sadguru will appear in person, for example, discussing how we will recognize the sadguru. I thought the position in yoga is that brahma is the only guru, and he has appeared only twice, once as Shiva and once as Krishna. So, that would make it infeasible for him to appear as a sadguru in front of us, isn't it? I thought taraka brahma in the sahasrara chakra is the one guiding each of us.

**Full Answer:**  
So it is true that Taraka Brahma takes physical form. But the sense of these paragraphs is not that He is always present in physical form. As it states, "At His own instance, when needed He takes human form for a predetermined period, for the welfare of living beings." So, examples of this are Lord Shiva and Lord Krsna. And they have given the science of yoga so that appropriately trained individuals, human beings, can teach it to those who have sincere desire to come to know the Infinite Entity. And, both when Taraka Brahma is present in physical form and when He is not-- at all times He is present in the sahasrara cakra of each and every human being, waiting for the spiritual yearning to awaken in them.
