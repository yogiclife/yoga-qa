**Original Question:**  
Clarification by acharya ji.

**Full Answer:**  
When one speaks about bhakti, one needs to make it very clear that bhakti can only be for Parama Purusa, and never for a physical entity like a human being or for a country etc. At the beginning of the presentation you said that bhakti means devotion, and then said something about how devotion is love and in the generic sense can be for a physical person or for the Infinite Entity. It needs to be made very clear that bhakti can never be for a person or country etc. Indeed, in the absence of the spiritual bhava of brahmacarya in which one does adhyaropa of spiritual love upon a gunanvita manifestation of purusa such as a child or another person, there is actually no such thing in the manifest world as love at all: worldly relations in the absence of the spiritual feeling of brahmacarya just described, are merely moha and are based on selfishness and attachment. Love is something selfless and can only be for God. Everything else is just moha.

The sister who volunteered to explain what she'd understood after you explained the five types of bhakti also repeated the same idea as she began, i.e. that devotion can be for a worldly entity such as a person, or can be for the Supreme Entity. But devotion can only be for Parama Purusa. Devotion means bhakti. And if anyone is using the word devotion to mean anything else, then it really isn't relevant to this conversation.
