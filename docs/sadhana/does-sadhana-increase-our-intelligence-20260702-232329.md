**Original Question:**  
Does shadhna increase our intelligence? If yes, then there are many people like terrorist who have high intelligence. Does it mean that they are more close to Parama Purusha?

**Full Answer:**  
People are born having the faculty of intellect, and by birth some have more of it and some have less. With regard to its development and use, there are two factors:
1. Intellect is a tool that can be used or abused. That is, depending upon whether one is guided by manava dharma or (abuse of) pashu dharma in one's life, one may or may not make proper use of one's intellect. So one may be born with a very sharp intellect, but be guided by crude samskaras to use this intellect for negative purposes. There is no shortage of such people. Their intellect is called kuta-buddhi. They are conniving and very negative people, very dangerous. Whereas those who properly use their intellect, that intellect is su-buddhi. Humans have free will, so they can use their intellect as they like. So this is regarding negative use of intellect. And by this, in response to your question, that merely having sharp intellect does not at all mean that one is close to Parama Purusa. By their free will they made negative use of their intellect, and they will get reborn as stones.
2. Regarding the effect of sadhana on one's intellect: Sadhana has two effects on intellect. (a) Firstly, sadhana will guide one to use this tool of intellect in the proper way, as su-buddhi. Inspired by sadhana, one will use one's intellect for atma-moksa and jagat-hita. (b) Secondly, through the practice of sadhana, intellect becomes concentrated and one-pointed-- it becomes "pinnacled intellect". The buddhi becomes ekagracitta (एकाग्रचित्त). This pointed intellect is far more powerful and insightful than that very same intellect before having started the practice of sadhana. So buddhi surely becomes developed through sadhana.
So by reading the above two points one can understand that by point #2, any given person, their intellect will be powerfully developed by doing sadhana. But this does not mean that all persons having sharp intellect are "close to Parama Purusa", as discussed in point #1.
