**Original Question:**  
Is it correct to say that when we practice Shadhna our reflected consciousness improved and which helps to focus more on our education ?

**Full Answer:**  
Yes:
1. By following the yogic life the mind becomes more pure, just as the puddle of water which had mud in it and now is becoming clear. Initially the reflecting of the moon was not at all clear in the muddy water, and as the water is cleaned it begins to reflect the moon more and more clearly. This is what happens when the human being begins to follow the yogic life, including all its various aspects, and most importantly sadhana (which of course requires the proper food, upavasa, asanas etc to be able to be done properly). So through the practice of sadhana, the reflection of consciousness increases.
2. By following the yogic life and doing sadhana, the mind becomes one-pointed i.e. it becomes concentrated. As it learns to focus on the Supreme Consciousness, the mind becomes a very powerful tool. And that tool i.e. the mind, when such a mind is directed toward academic studies it naturally excels.
