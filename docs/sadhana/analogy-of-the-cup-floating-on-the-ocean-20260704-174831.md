**Original Question:**  
Analogy of the cup floating on the ocean

**Full Answer:**  
When you explain the analogy of the cup floating on the ocean, rather than say that "when the cup breaks, the water inside and outside the cup become one", instead of "breaks" a more accurate phrase is "when the cup is removed". It is not that the cup remains there in a broken state. Rather, the cup is gone-- it merges into the ocean.  Ashok ji's way of expressing it is quite good, that the cup itself is made of frozen water and so, when the cup melts it merges with the water in the ocean, as does the water that was in the cup. The cup, being the unit mind, melts through the process of sadhana.
