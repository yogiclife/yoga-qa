**Original Question:**  
One question is there regarding the spiritual name. If we are trying to think Param Purusha beyond name and form, how does a spiritual name help?

**Full Answer:**  
So as we were saying yesterday, having a spiritual name is deeply meaningful. When one has a name which means Parama Purusa, then every time the name is used it reminds both those speaking the name and those hearing it, that this person is the gunanvita, gunayukta manifestation of Parama Purusa Himself. 

This morning I received a question:  "One question is there regarding the spiritual name. If we are trying to think Param Purusha beyond name and form, how does a spiritual name help?"

Here the point is that we live in the world, and interact with all the objects of the world. So while it is true that the ultimate goal of life is to merge in Nirguna Brahma, but saying thus does not mean, for example, that we will not take food because it has a name and form, or that we will not speak with anyone as they have name or form. Things in the world have name and form, and we have to interact with them to survive-- and so it becomes a question of how we should interact with them. And the answer is: Through the approach of brahmacarya or madhuvidya, i.e. to practice  the bhava that each of the objects of the world is a gunanvita rupa of Parama Purusa. By so doing we will not develop attachment for these things, and will experience them as manifestations of Brahma. 

And the very same idea applies to ourselves as well. The whole point is to realize that "aham Brahma asmi". So although we ourselves have name and form, we should feel that we are Brahma, part of that vast ocean of consciousness. So just as we practice adhyaropa of Brahma-bhava with the things of the world, we practice it with ourselves also. Because it is indeed true! Each of us is the gunanvita rupa of Parama Purusa, and it is just a question of realizing it. And that we do every day in our practice of sadhana. And for all the hours when we are not doing sadhana, we should be engaged in the same bhava, through all the tools available to us. One is ardha-iishvarapranidhana, and another tool is to have a spiritual name, which reminds us every time it is used that we are Brahma only. It is a beautiful tool, as we have to have some name-- so why not have it be a name that means I am Brahma? This is indeed so helpful. In contrast to a worldly name which reminds us of our worldly identity, a spiritual name reminds us that "I am Brahma". It frees us from the attachments to this world and reminds us every time we hear it of our true identity. 

Ask those who have a spiritual name about their experience, and they will tell you how beautiful an experience it is!
