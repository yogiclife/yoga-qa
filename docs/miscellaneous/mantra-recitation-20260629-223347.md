Clarification provided by Acharya ji

**Full Answer:**  
That sister's question regarding the "mantras" she recites every day as to which is more important--the pronunciation or the bhava she takes while reciting them, the question is a bit of a sensitive question for, as you know, what she is doing is itself really not proper. She is asking whether her pronunciation or her bhava is more important, but the very activity she is involved in is misguided. The entire purpose for which she is reciting these shlokas is very different from the purpose of yoga sadhana. For instance, the very example she gave-- that of a person sitting before "Mata Lakshmi" reciting a mantra with the goal of getting more money. She herself gave that example, asking whether in such a situation pronunciation is more important or bhava. So when one has such a goal as that of getting money, then the bhava taken while reciting the shloka is surely not going to be spiritual in nature. In sum, the reason her question is sensitive is that her whole practice is misguided, and in that scenario it does not matter regarding the pronunciation vs the bhava, best would be to just stop doing those things and come to the shivir and learn the yogic life. By the way she is talking it is apparent that she does not yet have a clear idea of what spirituality is, what its goal is.
