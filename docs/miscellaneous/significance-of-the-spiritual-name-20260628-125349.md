**Full Answer:**  
Another important point regards the spiritual name. The time when one learns yoga sadhana is a very special time, as it marks the time when one begins one's journey from 10:00 to 12:00, culminating in becoming one with Parama Purusa. So along with this, it is an auspicious time for receiving a spiritual name. A spiritual name will have the same first letter as one's birth name, and will have the meaning of Parama Purusa. 

That way when people call you, hearing the name is a reminder that you are That Entity. अहं ब्रह्मास्मि इति भावः. So it is highly beneficial to the person having the name.

And as well it is highly beneficial to those who call you by this name, as they are also reminded that the person they are addressing is the gunanvit rup of Parama Purusa, and that very Entity resides within them in the form of jiivatma.

So in this way the spiritual name vibrates all who use it-- both those who speak it, and those who hear it.

A number of the attendees at the shibiram have spiritual names: Vidhatrii ji, Nitya ji, Mayadhiish ji, Hrdayesh ji, Vishveshvarii ji, Rtambhara ji, Onkar ji and Nitya ji's two children Taran ji and Cinmayii ji, Istadev ji's son Nityam ji. Shubham ji has requested a spiritual name and we will be giving one to her soon.

If any others of you who attended the yoga shibiram would like a spiritual name, just let me know. It is a very powerful way of marking the start of one's spiritual journey, as it gives one a new identity-- that I am a gunanvit rup of Parama Purusa Himself.

Those who have been doing sadhana for some time already are also encouraged and welcome to request a spiritual name. It signifies a spiritual revolutionary spirit, that one does not have attachments in this worldly life and one is ready to move ahead boldly on the dharmic path toward merger with Parama Purusa. As, attachment to one's worldly name is common to all and indicates attachment to one's worldly identity and worldly achievements. Taking on a spiritual name marks a movement apart from and beyond the worldly path and a readiness to embrace a fresh, new identity as a gunanvit rup of Parama Purusa Himself, moving toward becoming one with Him.
