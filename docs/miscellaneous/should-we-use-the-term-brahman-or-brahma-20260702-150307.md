**Original Question:**  
2nd doubt was regarding the word “brahma” ..what is the correct pronunciation Brahman or Brahma .

**Full Answer:**  
So the word ब्रह्मन्‌ has two forms, a pumlinga form and a napumsakalinga form. The pumlinga form in ekavacana prathamAvibhakti is ब्रह्मा and means that aspect of PP who creates the universe. The napumsakalinga form in ekavacana prathamAvibhakti is ब्रह्म and refers to Parama Purusa in His totality, He who is the combined union of Purusa and Prakrti. 

Now, the prAtipadikam of BOTH the forms is ब्रह्मन्‌. So it can never be helpful to distinguish between the two by calling one "ब्रह्मन्‌". Both are ब्रह्मन्‌. This is a long-standing mistake in India, particularly in South India, and particularly those who teach and study vedanta. This usage is wrong in its very root, as both the forms have the same prAtipadikam-- "ब्रह्मन्‌".  Only those who do not know any Samskritam could have created such a usage.

The only proper way to distinguish between the two is to use their ekavacana prathamAvibhakti form: ब्रह्मा and ब्रह्म. 

ब्रह्मा means that aspect of PP who creates the universe. ब्रह्म means the totality of Parama Purusa.

[PP: Parama purusha]
