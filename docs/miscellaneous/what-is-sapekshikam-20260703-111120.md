**Original Question:**  
what sapeksika means, and you explained rightly that it means "relative"

**Full Answer:**  
Going forward, it is also helpful if we explain relative to what. When we did not explain that, then became confused as to what the difference is between something "relative" and something "subjective" or random. The word "subjective" is used by people to indicate something that is merely according to their whim, i.e. essentially without any rhyme or reason. Whereas "relative" means relative to a particular value system. Someone may subscribe to a value system which says that if you can cheat others for your benefit, then you should do so-- after all, by that way you are gaining money and that is the goal. Whereas in contrast, another may say that one should follow the guidelines of manava dharma, as that leads to universal welfare. So what we see here is that someone's judgement as to right and wrong is sapekshika or relative to their value system. By explaining it this way it will not be confusing that 'relative' is the same as 'subjective' or acting on a whim.
