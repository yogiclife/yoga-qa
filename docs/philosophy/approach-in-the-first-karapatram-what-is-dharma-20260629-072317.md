**Full Answer:**  
The approach in the first karapatram - What is Dharma ?is one of going from known to unknown, from ज्ञात to अज्ञात, from paricita to aparicita-- This is the main guiding principle for the structure of this first karapatram. The second section starts from seeing a book which is a very familiar experience that all have had, explains how that happens and on that basis of this generates an explanation of mind and its parts, then from there to the source of mind which is jiivatma, and from there to Paramatma. So the final paragraph is the culmination of this process, and as such uses the concepts generated about mind and unit consciousness to explain what Cosmic Consciousness is. And ultimately, as you pointed out, one then becomes able to understand conceptually what Cosmic Consciousness really is and that it is not merely the addition of all the units but is more than that. And yet the concept of it being the aggregate of all the unit consciousnesses, and not some human with powerful hands and feet, is key to developing that proper understanding.
