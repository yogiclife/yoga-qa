**Original Question:**  
We will later learn yogic ethics (yama and niyama). It can difficult to practice them when the situation is tough. The theory we study will be helpful then. 

Yogic ethics is a preliminary step before asana and dhyana in the traditional sequence of learning. So, the theory has practical benefits in enabling effective practice of sadhana. 

Sadhana without ethics can by dangerous. The most important aspect of ethics is ishvara pranidhana — sharanagati. For that, we need to understand who God is and who we are. Theory is helpful there too.

**Full Answer:**  
So there are three important reasons for philosophy:
1. To ensure one is on the proper path;
2. To understand how best to move ahead on the path;
3. To be able to explain to others (those one lives with, one's friends, and others) about why they too should move ahead on this path.
At the beginning especially, point #1 is extremely important. Everyone in the world lives their life according to a particular philosophy, whether they know it or not. Most people live according to the guidelines of materialism, and a substantial number of people live according to the guidelines of one or another religion. Take for example those who killed the 26 tourists in Kashmir yesterday; they surely thought they were doing the right thing, otherwise they wouldn't have done it. Their lives are guided by a certain philosophy. One may say that 99% of the people in the world are living according to one or another dogmatic philosophy. And the person who is not attending your class because there is "too much theory", she also lives her life in accordance with a particular philosophy-- and that philosophy is most assuredly a dogmatic one. Without even meeting her I can assure you that this is the case. In comparison with what she could be doing in her life, she is just wasting the time in one or another dogmatic activity. Either materialism or religious dogma, it is one of the two. And in the end, religious dogma itself also comes under the aegis of materialism. The bottom line is that she has not found the proper path in her life-- and by attending your class her life can be dramatically changed and improved as she will shift from animal life to human life.
