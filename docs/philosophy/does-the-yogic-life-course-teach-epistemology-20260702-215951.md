**Full Answer:**  
Regarding epistemology: although we do not use the word as such, but the concept is very much present. If they stay with us, they will come to understand very clearly (1) the value and importance given to rationality and proper use of rationality and viveka in deciding what to embrace and what to reject in this world; (2) the idea that one is not to accept blind beliefs (3) the role of intuition in epistemology. That is, how is one to decide to accept the practice of yogasadhana as a path toward truth? That cannot be proven via external tools or modalities. It requires an internal feeling; and that internal feeling is yet something entirely different from the blind belief involved in the acceptance of dogma. Rather, here we are talking about the faculty of bodhi or intuition. And that is very, very different from blind belief or blind acceptance. All of these are concepts related to how we come to know something. And that is what epistemology is all about.
