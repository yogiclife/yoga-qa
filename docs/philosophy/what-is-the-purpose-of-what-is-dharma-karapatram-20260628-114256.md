**Original Question:**  
Clarification provided by Acharya ji

**Full Answer:**  
Remember that this first karapatram "What is Dharma" is an introductory karapatram designed to (1) teach the concept of manava dharma, and (2) teach the concept of Parama Puruśa using the model of seeing a book. People come to our yogic life class carrying a lot of baggage from their childhood, their upbringing, and the society they live in, regarding the two points of (1) dharma and (2) God. So this initial karapatram has a huge job to do. If people come out the other side with a new idea in their mind as to what dharma is as well as what God is, then it has done its job. In the final paragraph it includes all these various words you enlisted (bhagavan, paramatman, universal soul, bhumacaetanya, cosmic consciousness, brahma) with the goal of completing the task it has set out to do, that of making clear that there are no other entities under these various names-- they all refer to what we have taken efforts to present before the learner. That is, there is the jiivatma as developed in the sequence of seeing a book, and the collective of all the jiivatmas is that Parama Puruśa, by whatever name one may use. There is nothing else. These are all names for the collective of consciousness. If people have understood and accepted this in their heart, then the job is done. They stop thinking of bhagavan as some figure with huge hands and feet, they stop thinking of brahma as something else, paramatma as something else, and consciousness as something else. The canvas has been cleaned and a new perspective brought. If that is achieved, it is a huge achievement. Then going forward the refinements can gradually be added to which you refer-- that comes later. But first let them grasp this huge conception of the individual unit consciousness, and the collective of all these being the Cosmic Consciousness, known by all these various names.
