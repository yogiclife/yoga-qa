**Original Question:**  
The last sentence of the first handout mentions "This shows that bhagavan does exist and it exists as paramatman or universal soul, bhumacaetanya or cosmic consciousness, or brahma, the eternal blessedness". Does this indicate that brahma means eternal blessedness? Does 'brahma' arise from बृहँ वृद्धौ (to grow, to prosper)? 

**Full Answer:**  
Don't be concerned about the term "eternal blessedness"--it is English translation, and the sense is that of सच्चिदानन्द । Yoga: You ask about how the word 'brahma' arises. The answer is-- बृहत्त्वाद्ब्रह्म  बृंहणत्वादद्ब्रह्म । The meaning is, Brahma is that Entity who (1) is Itself बृहत्‌, and (2) It makes others बृहत्‌ | Brahma is Himself vast, infinite, and those who imbibe His bhavana, He makes them vast, infinite as well. Hence He is known as Brahma.
