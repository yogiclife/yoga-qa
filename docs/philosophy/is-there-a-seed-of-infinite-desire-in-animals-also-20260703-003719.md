**Original Question:**  
The beejam of अनन्तैषणा should be in animals also if humans evolved from animals. So how can we say it is the dharma of humans only? 

**Full Answer:**  
Anyhow, the main point regarding Nyaya shastra is that those Naeyayakas write all sorts of things, and in the end it is all imagined. They are claiming to describe the indriya-grAhya jagat, and they do not use their indriyas or any tools at all to test their hypotheses-- it is all imagined. And it is not as though they are doing sadhana and getting the knowledge internally either. They are just crude, dry intellectuals -- intelligentsia as they are called -- imagining what they think could be the case. So for example, it is not at all true that when a dravya first comes into existence it has no gunas. What a concept! How could a dravya ever be nirguna? Only Nirguna Purusa is nirguna, and that happens where there is no srsti. All dravyas have gunas. Everything in the vishva is saguna. What a silly concept, that anything in the srsti is nirguna, even for a second. 

And also-- no need to try to work out ways in which yoga and nyaya work together. Yoga is the true path. Nyaya is not at all a path, and its description of the universe is so fatally flawed that it is hardly worth considering. 

Move ahead, and focus on our yogic teachings as you are doing. The nyaya can be mentioned here and there if you think it helpful, but don't hesitate to point out its faults also. People should not be misguided by it.
Whatever it may be, his mind is full of dogmatic teachings. He should try to follow along and open up his mind. We will point out his mistakes in a psychological way, so that his ego is not feeling injured. That way he will be able to remain in the class.

Regarding the need to focus on the substance of the teaching rather than who said it, we can use the following shloka--

Yuktiyuktamupádeyaḿ vacanaḿ bálakádapi;
Anyaḿtrńavat tyájyamapyayuktam Padmajanmanah.

“If even a young boy says something logical, it should be accepted, and if the Supreme Creator Brahmá says something illogical, it should be rejected as rubbish.”
The above was in reply to Swami Ji's (and anyone else's) question as to who the pravartak is.
Students should focus on the substance ie on the actual subject material.
