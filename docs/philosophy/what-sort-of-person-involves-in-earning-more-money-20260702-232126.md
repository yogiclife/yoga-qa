**Original Question:**  
What sort of person involves in earning more money than what is required to meet their needs?

**Full Answer:**  
For example, if we had explained that some people involve in earning much more money than they need, whereas some people only involve in earning the amount of money to meet their needs. One student asked what sort of person is it who does the latter? In the process of answering, if we explained that earning an amount of money which far exceeds one's needs has no practical use in our life and actually is a kind of animal dharma. And then he asked, is it clear? The student replied, "yes". But I do not think the student could have understood. To make this point clear, it would need to be explained why this is animal dharma. After all, animals do not use money at all. By "animal dharma" here we mean the practice of acquiring worldly things to fulfill one's worldly needs. Money is such a thing which does fulfill worldly needs. But over-acquisition of money -- just as over-acquisition of food, is abuse of animal dharma. And including the word "abuse" here is extremely important. It is not merely animal dharma-- it is abuse of animal dharma, which is done because of not understanding that one has an infinite desire, and unknowingly applying ones infinite desire to the field of animal desire, which results in its abuse.
