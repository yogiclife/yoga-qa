**Original Question:**  
I am still trying to understand consciousness — Purusha — properly. In normal life, I think of consciousness as an abstraction, my ability to be aware that I exist. But when describing purusha, I recall that it is a substance. So, it is probably a little different from what I think of as consciousness. I am somewhat comfortable with not understanding it fully; it is probably beyond the mind and words. 

But I would like to understand our choice of calling it consciousness. Is it to distinguish the spiritual perspective from materialistic ones?

Materialists think of consciousness as an emergent property of inert matter. Are we saying that consciousness cannot come from something inert? Since we are conscious and a unit of purusha, purusha cannot be inert. So, we call purusha as consciousness to indicate that our belief that consciousness arises from an underlying substance that itself is consciousness, rather than inert matter?

Is the Sanskrit word ‘purusha’ related to the English translation ‘consciousness’? I could not see a connection and am wondering about the choice of this word to indicate purusha.

**Full Answer:**  
While the term consciousness is not totally unrelated to the notion of being "aware", it would not be proper to equate it with the worldly state of being "aware" of one's existence. Firstly, that is an adjective, whereas consciousness is a noun -- and not merely a noun in the sense of being bhAvArthe. There are nouns which are just the bhAva of an adjective, such as beautiful => beauty. Beauty is merely the bhAva of beautiful. But the word consciousness, in the sense in which we use it in yoga, is not like that. It refers, as you suggested, to a subtle substance. And purusha is the word for that substance in Samskrta. 

Materialists are completely off the mark regarding purusha/consciousness. Consciousness is, as you know the original substance from which everything in the universe is made. It is not something which is merely a state of existence -- the state of being conscious or aware -- which materialists believe emerges from the conglomerate of complex electrical pathways which they acknowledge as the brain, which they believe to be the source of functionality and intelligence.
