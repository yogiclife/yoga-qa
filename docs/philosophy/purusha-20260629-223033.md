**Original Question:**  
Clarification provided by Acharya ji

**Full Answer:**  
Purusa is not only unit consciousness, and it is not always or even mostly associated with unit mind. The stones, wood, fire, air is not linked with unit mind, but it is all made of purusa. The main point to emphasize here in the explanation is that everything is made of purusa: wood, iron, stone, cement, soil, trees, plants, air, clouds. And of course all the jiivas. But even in the jiivas, it is not only the unit consciousness which is made of purusa-- everyone's hands, feet, blood, intestines, lungs-- everything is made of purusa. That is what we mean by substrate. It is what everything is made of. Last point: as this is the case, that everything is made of purusa, as it is the substrate in everything, it will always take the ekavacana. Do not say "purusas". Purusa is a singular entity. There are not multiple purusas. There are multiple unit consciousnesses, but purusa is one. It is the ocean of purusa.
