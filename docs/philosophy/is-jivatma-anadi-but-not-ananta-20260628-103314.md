**Original Question:**  
I understand that it is eternal and so endless in time. But I thought it has an end in space. For example, my jivatma ends before yours starts. Or is space and time just a characteristic of the created world, and so not relevant in the spiritual realm where the pure atma resides?Is jivatma anadi but not ananta?

**Full Answer:**  
Jivatma is both anadi and ananta. It is just the name given to the portion of Paramatma which is assigned to/linked with the unit mind. It is purusa itself, and cannot be destroyed. hen you ask "Is jivatma anadi but not ananta?", your question is kAlika in nature, not daishika. Because "anadi" is a kAlika quality. I do not think you meant 'anadi' in a daishika sense. So the question is kAlika, and I gave you the answer in that very sense.
