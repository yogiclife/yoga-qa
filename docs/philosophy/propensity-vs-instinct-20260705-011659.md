**Original Question:**  
Clarification by Acharya ji

**Full Answer:**  
Vrtti means "propensity", not "instinct". The term सहजात-वृत्ति means "in-born instict". But vrtti does not mean instinct; rather, it means "propensity". It is not that all vrttis are inborn qualities. The environment in which one lives, and the lifestyle one follows, both of these have a very powerful impact on expression of vrttis in one's life. For an animal, their vrttis are sahajAta no doubt, but for a human being there are very few that are sahajAta -- and of those that are sahajAta, most disappear during infancy. One example is the Moro reflex, also called the startle reflex. This reflex is present at birth and disappears between 3 and 6 months of age.
