**Original Question:**  
"Quantitative difference" versus "Qualitative difference"

**Full Answer:**  
Regarding "quantitative difference" versus "qualitative difference", the main thing to make clear is that in "quantitative difference", all the jiivas being compared have the quality to some extent-- some have more of it, and some less. For example, with buddhi, all have buddhi to some extent. Some have more and some have less.  But ALL have it to some extent. So we cannot justify jati-bhed on this point, because all are having the guna to a lesser or greater extent.

Whereas in "qualitative difference", some of the jiivas have it, whereas others do not have it at all. It is a matter of all vs none. With the guna of having infinite desire, all humans have it, whereas no animal has it at all. It is not that animals have it to some extent; they do not have it at all. So here it is on/off, or all/none. This is the reason underlying jati bhed.
