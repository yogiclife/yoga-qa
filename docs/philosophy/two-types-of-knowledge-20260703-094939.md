**Original Question:**  
Two types of Knowledge

**Full Answer:**  
As we know, there are only two types of jinanam-- apara and para. Apara jinanam is worldly knowledge and para is Brahma jinana. Brahma jinanam is gotten only by sadhana. Sadhana is done only with Bhakti.

The jinanam which  Bhagini is referring to -- अद्वैतज्ञानम् -- is in the category of apara jinanam, not para jinanam. It is worldly knowledge, pustakiiya jinanam acquired by reading books. That jinanam is not at all needed for moving ahead on the spiritual path. What is needed is bhakti i.e. love for Parama Purusa. When one does bhakti-purna sadhana then only one gets Brahma-jinana. That Brahma-jinanam is an anubhuti which one gets only through bhakti-purna sadhana. It is the result of sadhana.

The अद्वैतज्ञानम् is bookish knowledge; it is just book knowledge, worldly knowledge. It is better thrown in the garbage bin. Some read lots of books on advaetavada due to which they now think they know everything and arre enlightened soul. Whereas actually he is a useless fellow, totally sunk in the darkness of ignorance. That is because all he has done his entire life is feed his ahamkara and swell up his small I-feeling like a balloon. A balloon which is hollow inside. This is the result of what Bhagini is talking about, because she thinks that one wing of the bird is bookish knowledge. And she doesn't understand what bhakti is-- she doesn't even recognize that one needs to learn sadhana, sit in sadhana and focus one's mind on Parama Purusa.

If someone does not do sadhana, does not do service, and only reads discourses, that is dangerous. If one implements what one learns in them one will move along the dharmic path. But it is not enough just to read about the roadmap-- one has to move ahead along the road. 

In the Hindu religion, people are taught that it is enough just to listen to and study the scriptures. This is useless and dangerous. By so doing one will end up as some egotistical pandit who thinks they know, when in reality they are totally ignorant.
