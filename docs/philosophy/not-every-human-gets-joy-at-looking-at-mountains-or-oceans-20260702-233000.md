**Original Question:**  
People who live near the ocean or fishermen who are at the sea all the time do not get joy from looking at the ocean or mountain. Also if somebody is living in the midst of the mountains then it is routine for him and he does not get joy. How do we explain this as not every human gets joy from seeing the mountains or oceans. 

**Full Answer:**  
- This comment entirely misses the point of our main subject. Natural surroundings such as a huge mountain or the ocean are a surrogate for the infinite. They are not infinite, but they feel very big indeed and as such they inspire a feeling within which is common to all human beings. If a fisherman is used to the ocean or a person living on a mountain is used to it and doesn't get inspired by it, that is only because they got used to the scenery and so it no longer inspires the feeling of the infinite within. But if the fisherman has never been to the Himalayas and gets the opportunity to go there, he may get that very thrill down his spine when he sees the mountain for the first time. And similarly with the mountain dweller who sees the ocean for the first time.

- Additionally, there may be some people who have indeed become so crudified by their abuse of animal dharma, by their crude lifestyle, and as a result become so distanced from their manava dharma that they are unable to feel the thrill of the infinite within them. That is only because they have degenerated themselves so much that they can no longer respond as a human would. But the great, great majority of people do still relate with the infinite desire within them and get inspired when they see things which are immense. And those who no longer do, likely would have when they were young and as yet had not degenerated themselves so badly.
