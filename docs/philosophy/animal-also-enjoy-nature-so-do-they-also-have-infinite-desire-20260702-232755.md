**Original Question:**  
Peacocks also get joy from rains. They start to dance during the rains and get joy. So how are we saying that only humans get unique joy from seeing the ocean, mountains etc. Animals also get joy from the nature.  

**Full Answer:**  
- It is true that animals get joy from nature. Not only peacocks, but all the animals do. It is their natural surroundings. If one brings one's pet dog to the forest or to the seashore they will get quite excited and start frolicking and running around in delight. 
- But such enjoyment of nature has nothing to do with a desire for the infinite. It is just the natural instinctive play of animals. 
- Human beings go to the ocean or to the mountain and get a thrill down their spine due to the immensity, because it gives them a feeling of something big -- really big. Similarly, people lie on the ground and look up at the sky at night and become amazed and stare in wonder at the immensity of it all. Animals do stare up at the night sky in amazement. 
- So the reason for humans enjoying a huge mountain or ocean or the night sky is very different from the enjoyment which animals get. The experience as well as the reason behind it is totally different and cannot be compared. Animals know nothing of the infinite and do not have a yearning for it, whereas it is the essential and defining characteristic of what it is to be human.
