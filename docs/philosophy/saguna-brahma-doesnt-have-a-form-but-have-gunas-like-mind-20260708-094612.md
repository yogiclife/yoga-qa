**Original Question:**  
Can we say saguna brahma doesn’t have a form but have gunas like mind? How are the people who have got habituated to this so called saguna upasana where they impose a roopa and goona to the name and form they worship? Coz some people are so deeply got connected to those forms say shiva and linga or shakti worship in the form of mother..how can someone come out of those long standing ritualistic habits. Telling the portraits or idols are not worth of focus points for spiritual uplifting can create aversion to their spiritual interest.

**Full Answer:**  
Very good questions! Indeed.



1. Saguna Brahma is Cosmic Mind. It is suksma i.e. does not admit of tanmatras. So yes, SB is gunAnivita consciousness, yet does not have any form.



2. The religious dogmas are like the material engagements of the materialists: both are like the mother's car keys which the baby is playing with. If the baby is given a better toy, he/she will drop the car keys and start playing with the better toy. Because people who have become wedded to religious dogmas did not accept those dogmas on the basis of rationality and reasoning, they will not be able to reject them on the basis of rationality and reasoning either. The attraction they have to their rituals and murtis is based solely on sentiment, and not on reason. And so their rejection of these things will also come on the basis of sentiment rather than reason.



The sentiment will be for something higher. When they learn the yogic life and srsti cakra, yogic practices, and ultimately sadhana, if they engage in practicing these things they will develop a very high-minded and strong sentiment, a strong attraction for them. And on the basis of that attraction, on the basis of the spiritual ananda, spiritual bliss they feel, they will lose their attraction for the religious dogmas which used to be so precious for them. 



So bottom line: No need to engage in discussion with your students regarding religious dogma. Just teach them the correct ideas and correct approach-- with time they will naturally lose interest in thse old things which they were taught to love back in their childhood.
