**Original Question:**  
Can AI house infinite desire?

**Full Answer:**  
Over time, humans will undoubtedly progress, leading to changes in body/brain structure, intelligence, and so forth. However, this does not imply the emergence of a new Shreni (plants, animals, humans). Humans possess everything necessary to merge with the Supreme Being — human body and human mind — which is sufficient to fulfill the purpose of our existence." This is indeed the bottom line. As human beings complete the cycle of creation, human beings will continue to develop and evolve as they have done on this planet earth-- but there will never be a more developed being than the human being. There is no need for it. Human beings, by their evolved mind which houses an infinite desire, are the beings who complete the srsti cakra. That is the bottom line. As such, Madhuratama ji is correct, that "In terms of mind, the highest evolution is already achieved, because the human mind is capable of realizing the infinite, and since we know that there is nothing greater than infinite, this means that human mind is the most capable mind in the entire universe." And as the purpose of the brain is to house the mind, human beings will always have the most developed brain as well. Human beings have been developing since they first took their first steps in this universe, and they will continue to develop and evolve. But there will never be a more evolved being than the human being, who has an infinite desire, does sadhana, merges into and becomes one with Parama Purusa, and thus completes the srsti cakra. Less evolved beings were needed so as to gradually reach to 10:00, and, from there, human beings, having the ability to complete the srsti cakra, are all that is needed and as such there cannot ever be any more evolved being than the human being.

The basic point is very simple: AI is just a machine. It is a tool made by human beings. No matter how developed it becomes, it is a mere machine and cannot have any desires at all, what to speak of a desire for the infinite. It is itself ksititattva, has no mind, and if we are to locate it in srsti cakra we will place it at 6:00, in the same location as stones, iron, and other items of ksititattva.  

The very first step in evolution is for something to emerge from ksititattva under congenial conditions, such that the shakti it contains takes the form of life. It must emerge from ksititattva to become alive-- to become a jiiva. And on this point there is absolutely no question of AI being "alive". 

Under congenial conditions, the kendratiga and kendranuga shakti-- the forces acting upon and within the ksititattva which we have discussed together in many satsang sessions result in the emergence of life from stone (rather than the explosion of the stone back into painca tattva and back to 3:00). And when this happens, we get a one-celled organism which has dasha práńáh (the ten vital energies), as a result of which the metabolic processes of life begin in that jiiva, and due to the impact of sattvaguna, as the ksititattva becomes a jiiva, from within the ksititattva emerges mind and associated jiivatma which is linked with it from the infinite pool of shuddha purusa or consciousness. From then begins the process of samgharsa and samiiti which results in the evolution into more and more developed life forms. A computer (i.e. AI) has not even begun any of this. It is completely sthula, and as such is located at 6:00 in srsti cakra. It does not have dasha práńáh, it does not have any metabolic processes within it, it does not eat to get energy, it does not require water or oxygen, it has no brain, no mind, and no atma. It is just ksititattva, arranged by human beings in such a way that when supplied with electric power it begins to do some work as any other electric tool.

So in sum this is a very simple matter for a sadhaka. A computer, no matter how developed, is nirjiiva-- it has no práńáh, no mind, no atma, no desires, and as such is located at 6:00 in srsti cakra. There is no question of its even being alive, what to speak of having desires or moving towards the infinite. This is a very simple matter, and Hrdayesh ji you can explain this to your class if you like. Hariharan was thinking this is a complicated matter, but it is not at all complicated. Machines can never have any role in pratisaincara-dhara. Made of iron, plastic, and other items of ksititattva they are located at 6:00, awaiting congenial conditions for life to emerge out of their crude components. 

Insects and mice are more advanced on the path of moving toward the infinite compared to any computer, as computers haven't even begun the journey of pratisaincara-dhara.
