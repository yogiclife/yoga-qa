**Full Answer:**  
So there are two ब्रह्मन्‌ shabdas. One is napumsakalinga, and one is pumlinga. Although it is true that there is a nominative form 'ब्रह्मा',  but that is not the form we are using here. The two forms have different meanings, so this differentiation is important.  

Two Brahman shabdas 
(a) ब्रह्मन् नकारान्तः, नपुंसकलिङ्गः
प्रथमा-भक्तिः	ब्रह्म	ब्रह्मणी	ब्रह्माणि
(b) ब्रह्मन् नकारान्तः, पुंलिङ्गः
प्रथमा-भक्तिः	ब्रह्मा	ब्रह्माणौ	ब्रह्माणः

The Brahma which is the collective of Purusa and Prakrti is the first of the two listed above, i.e. the napumsakalinga shabda. Its nominative form is ब्रह्म. This is the shabda we are using throughout this series of karapatrams. Nirguna Brahma, Saguna Brahma, Taraka Brahma-- all these usages are of ब्रह्म only. 

The second shabda, the pumlinga shabda, has the nominative form ब्रह्मा. So ब्रह्म as a Singular Entity has three phases within it which are delineated by the role played in each phase-- the Creator, the Maintaner, and the Withdrawer/Destroyer. These phases receive the names ब्रह्मा, विष्णुः, महेश्वरः । Although treated in religion as three distinct entities, they are not at all so but rather names for the three roles played by ब्रह्म |
