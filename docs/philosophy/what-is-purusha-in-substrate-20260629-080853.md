**Original Question:**  
Clarification by Acharya ji

**Full Answer:**  
 here "substrate" means that from which something is made. So for example, an 8 year old boy is standing in front of me. He is made of purusa. Purusa is the ingredient out of which everything is made. But although it is a fact that the boy is made of purusa, that does not mean that we should do sastaunga pranama, dandavat pranam before him and start ideating on the boy with hopes of getting moksa. Because the boy is not shuddha purusa and also, not kendrit purusa. So in the same way, we will not do puja in front of stones, and we will not do puja to the air, and we will not do "surya namaskar". There is an Entity, Parama Purusa. And He is the controller of everything. Yes, He is present everywhere. But "purusa" is the water in the ocean, as Ashok ji has said here. Whereas, Parama Purusa is the ocean itself.
