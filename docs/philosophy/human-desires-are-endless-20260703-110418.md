**Original Question:**  
How it is the humans' desires are endless, whereas animals' desires are not. She said that animals will go on being hungry for food their entire lives, and humans as well

**Full Answer:**  
Here the point is this: animals' desire for food is *recurring, but not endless* . Recurring and endless are not the same thing. They do become hungry every day, yes-- but on any given day as soon as their stomach is full, the hunger and desire to eat ends. So their desire for food is finite. And that desire recurs every day, but on any given day it ends when the stomach is full. Whereas the human has an infinite desire, and so at any given mealtime they apply their infinite desire to the activity of taking food and that causes them to overeat, which is something an animal living in the wild will never do. So the cause of Bhagini's confusion was the need to understand the *difference between a recurring but finite desire, versus an infinite or endless desire* (which, when applied to a finite thing such as food, results in overconsumption).
