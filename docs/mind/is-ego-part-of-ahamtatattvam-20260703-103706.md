**Original Question:**  
"Is Ahamkara (ego) a part of Mahattatva or Ahamtattva?", and you say that "the ego exists because of Mahattatva, while Ahamtattva is merely the doer."

**Full Answer:**  
So, ultimately, the truth is that if Mahattatva were not developed, Ahamkara (ego) would not exist—however, this ego is fully present within Ahamtattva. Mahattatva is purely the "I feeling" (the simple sense of existence). "I did this work"—this awareness does not belong to Mahattatva, but rather to Ahamtattva.

Thinking is also an action. Therefore, yes, Ahamtattva is the doer, and within that 'act of doing', thinking is also included. A human being's personality resides in Ahamtattva. Whether someone is humble or arrogant—all of this is in Ahamtattva. The experiencing of Samskaras (reactive momenta/past impressions) also takes place in Ahamtattva. And it is that very Ahamtattva which desires credit for the work it has done.
