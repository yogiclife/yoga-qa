**Original Question:**  
When we say that due to a developed Mahattattva, we are aware of our actions and we become responsible for them too. Whereas animals are not held responsible for their actions. Example: a cat doesn't know it's eating, it is just eating. Whereas we know : I know I am eating. So, I know I am doing whatever it is that i do. So, I know  I am - (existing) I am aware of my existence, isn't  it ?

**Full Answer:**  
Everything you are describing here is happening in the ahamtattva -- due to the presence of the developed mahattattva that a human being has.



The feeling in mahattattva is a feeling only. That feeling gives the ahamtattva of a human being a whole new dimension. It becomes the doer of its actions. Whereas the ahamtattva of an animal is not the "doer of its actions".



The "I-feeling" is just that -- a feeling. As you are sitting in your home now, looking at your phone or your computer, you can *feel* that you are there. Animals do not have that feeling. There are just there. It is a big difference.
