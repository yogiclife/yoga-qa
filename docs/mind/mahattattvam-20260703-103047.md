**Original Question:**  
5th page second paragraph:
“Similarly AhamTatva only has the capacity to perform actions, it can only work. There is something to make it work. That something is Mahatttva..” Reading this, it feels like Ahamtattva is only like an instrument of Mahattatva; it is able to do this work only because Mahattatva exists.


**Full Answer:**  
The fact is that the capacity to act resides within Ahamtattva. Whether in animals or in humans, this Ahamtattva will only function when it receives the inspiration to act from an external corner.

In humans, Ahamtattva becomes the active "Doer I" only when the sense of "I" is handed over to it through the medium of Mahattatva. Once that happens, the ego enters into Ahamtattva, and based on that, it begins to work intensely. In animals, that Ahamtattva becomes active through their instincts. This is precisely why it is being said that "There must be something to make it work
