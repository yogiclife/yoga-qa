**Original Question:**  
Paragraph 8 of the handout on _8-Intuitional Practice and its Necessity_ mentions, " _The mind spread in chitta, aham tattva and maha tattva must gradually be withdrawn from them and then alone will it be concentrated. Thus the concentration of mind is nothing else but sadhana or intuitional practice which liberates units from the influence of Prakriti._ " I thought concentration is something the mind does. I am wondering how the mind would withdraw from the mahattattva. It looks like a description of nirvikalpa samadhi. From what I remember, the anu-mind is suspended in both savikalpa and nirvikalpa samadhi. So, the mind will no longer be concentrating on anything, isn't it? Paragraph 9 of the same handout mentions, "Concentration of mind is only Savikalpa Samadhi where the only feeling that remains is I am that." If the mind is suspended during savikalpa samadhi, then is the feeling something that arises in jeevatma. I assume the mind cannot feel because it is suspended.

**Full Answer:**  
You wrote, "I thought concentration is something the mind does." 



In order to reach the answer to your below query, first we need to understand what concentration is. People generally think that mental concentration refers to what you described above i.e. that it is something the mind does. For example, "His mind was fully concentrated on the math problem". But here in yoga, the term "concentration" has a deeper sense: it is a mental state, rather than something the mind does. We need to recall our key principle that, "As the mind thinks, so it becomes". The mind has no shape of its own, and takes the form of that about which it thinks. For that reason the following meaning is given for concentration of mind: "Steadying the vacillation of the mind and developing concentration of mind is the same thing." This absence of vacillation of mind can only be achieved when the vishaya of the mind is such an object in which there is no vacillation. And that object is only one-- shuddha purusa. When the mind does sadhana it takes as its visaya shuddha purusa and hence it becomes like that very shuddha purusa. Then only does the mind lose all vacillation and enter a state of concentration. When in the process of sadhana the citta withdraws into ahamtattva and ahamtattva withdraws into mahattattva, the mahattattva is still present but unaffected by the vacillations of ahamtattva and citta. This is savikalpa samadhi, the state of concentration of mind. As a result of focusing on the Ocean of Consciousness, the mind loses all its vacillation and become fully concentrated.
