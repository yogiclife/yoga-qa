**Original Question:**  
How can a practitioner (sadhaka) who is doing spiritual practice (sadhana) get involved in such crude work, meaning a magician's work?

**Full Answer:**  
So, all of this happens—until someone reaches the stage of Vashiikara, downfall (adhogati) is possible. People fall from the stages of Yataman, Vyatirek, and Ekendriya. Some declare themselves as gurus and open ashrams, some become magicians, and some perform magic in the guise of a "guru." Now, for those who have devotion (bhakti) and are surrendered to the Supreme Consciousness (Parama Purusa), this will never happen in their lives. But all kinds of people walk the path of sadhana. Some go upward, while others get trapped in ego and occult powers (siddhis) and fall. There are all types of people.

Another sister was asking how all this magic is done, how it happens. The answer is that it happens in two ways. One is the ordinary kind, which is done through sleight of hand and not through the mind. Many people do this; the mind is not involved here, and the audience is actually looking with their own eyes—it is purely a matter of hand agility, where the hand moves faster than the eye can see or catch. And the second is siddhi, which is a matter of the mind. Just as there are two types of Tantra—Spiritual Tantra, which we are engaged in, and the other is Avidya Tantra. These Avidya tantriks live in cremation grounds, eat raw meat, and do negative things using their minds—like using their mind to make stones pelt down on other people's roofs. So all of this happens through the mind; there is immense power in the mind. If the mind becomes single-pointed, one can do various kinds of harmful work—however, they will not get a human mind and human body again.
