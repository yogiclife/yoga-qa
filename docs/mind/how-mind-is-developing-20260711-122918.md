**Original Question:**  
 I thought kshiti would need to lose tamas for the ahamtattva to emerge, and for ahamtattva to lose rajas for mahattattva to emerge in the unit mind. That is, the unit ahamtattva would have no tamas and the unit mahattattva would have no rajas, isn’t it?



**Full Answer:**  
The thing to focus on really is that through struggle, the mind is developing. So although I suppose it would not be technically wrong to say the mind is "losing gunas", that really does not reflect the spirit of what is happening. What is happening is that the mind is developing. And so it is far better to describe the process in terms which reflect what is happening. The mind is not "losing" anything; it is rather *gaining* qualities-- it is becoming able to control the body and guide it to take actions according to its desire-- it tells the body to run and the body runs, it tells the voice to make a noise and a noise is made-- when ahamtattva becomes active then the mind has more qualities, not less. It is far more developed and as such has more qualities and characteristics. It is not losing gunas but rather gaining them as it develops. Yes, it is gradually becoming less bound, but the proper way to describe this is that the mind is developing. ‎
