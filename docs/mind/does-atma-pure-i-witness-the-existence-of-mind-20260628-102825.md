**Original Question:**  
Let us consider the sentence '*The "I"* _that gives me consciousness or the "I" which witnesses the existence of my mind_ and therein of the "I" which gives the feeling of "I exist" *is atman* or unit consciousness'. Is my following understanding correct. This sentence basically says the material in boldface: 'The "I" is atman'. The material in italics is describing that "I". (I assume 'consciousness' is not used in the sense of 'purusha' here, because nothing gives purusha; all is purusha. I take the 'me' here as being in the sense of the mind, and the atma is leading to the mind having thoughts and motivating activities, as any conscious being would have.) The part from 'therein' to 'exist' refers to mahattattva, which is a part of the mind. That is, the atma witnesses the mind and the mahattattva that is a component of the mind. Is my understanding correct?

**Full Answer:**  
Yes, all correct. And in the first sentence, "Without consciousness there can be no feeling of existence", the term 'consciousness' means purusa. Jivatma is both anadi and ananta. It is just the name given to the portion of Paramatma which is assigned to/linked with the unit mind. It is purusa itself, and cannot be destroyed.
