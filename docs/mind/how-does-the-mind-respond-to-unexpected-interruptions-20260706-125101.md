**Original Question:**  
Mahodaya, I was wondering how the ahamtattva knows which indriya it should order the chitta to pay attention to. 



Let us say I am reading a book — that happened because ahamtattva ordered so. Someone then makes a loud noise and I pay attention to the noise. How did ahamtattva know that there was a noise and the chitta should focus on it instead of reading the book?

**Full Answer:**  
There are multiple factors involved in a complex algorithm, which is a combination of interest and safety. Normally, interest dominates the algorithm. So if one is deeply involved in reading something, one will not pay attention to the backround noise in the room and so those sounds will not make it to the citta. But if the sound suddenly changes and becomes quite loud and dramatic, then the safety factor will take priority in the algorithm such that focus on the book gets disrupted, and the ahamtattva responds to the loud sound which gets brought to the citta in place of the visual pages of the book.



[Follow up question] I thought ahamtattva can perceive only the form taken by the chitta. So, it would not know that there is a sound unless chitta took that form. But chitta would take the form of the sound only if ahamtattva asked to listen to the shrotrendriya. But ahamtattva would not know that there was a sound because chitta has not taken that form. So, there appears to be a deadlock here; chitta and ahamtattva both need the other to take the first step.



[Answer to the follow up question] There is no such deadlock Ashok ji. 😊 There are various levels of reflex arcs in the nervous system. As I said, normally the algorithm is dominated by interest. But there are safety reflex arcs embedded in the system as well. That is why, for example, if one touches a pot which one did not know was hot, the hand jerks quickly away from the pot-- and only after that does one realize that, "Oh! The pot was hot." That reflex arc occurs at the level of the spinal cord-- the hand jerks away without the ahamtattva's involvement even. But as I said, there are various levels of reflex arc. Some involve the ahamtattva as well. If there is a loud sound, it may get forwarded to the citta out of pre-designation by the ahamtattva that if the sound is above a certain volume then it should get brought to the citta. Because one wants to be notified if something dangerous is happening. So all these various levels of input are programmed into the algorithm.
