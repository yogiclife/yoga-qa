**Original Question:**  
:“Without this I (Mahattatva) it is not possible to feel or see a book, even if under the influence of Ahamtattva Chitta takes the shape of book”.  Without Mahattatva, we cannot even look at a book... but in animals, Mahattatva is not developed, yet they are able to look at a book."

**Full Answer:**  
Animals have a great number of instincts, and on the strength of those, their Ahamtattva becomes active. Humans do not have that many instincts; they have very few. Therefore, if the sense of "I" were not handed over from the side of Mahattatva, Ahamtattva would just sit idle and would not be able to do anything. Once the "Doer I" of Ahamtattva becomes activated through the medium of Mahattatva, it becomes highly active—"I did this, I did that, tomorrow I will do this, and I will do that."
