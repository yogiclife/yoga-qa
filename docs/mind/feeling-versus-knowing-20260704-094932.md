**Original Question:**  
I am trying to understand 'Feeling versus knowing

**Full Answer:**  
"To feel" is akarmaka; "To know" is sakarmaka.



Said in English: "To feel" is an intransitive verb; it has no object. "To know" is a transitive verb. The knower is the subject, and that which is known is the object. "I feel good" -- there is no object. "I know Hindi" -- "I" is the subject, "Hindi" is the object. 



Feeling and knowing are very, very different things.
