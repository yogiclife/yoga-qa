**Original Question:**  
Functionality of the two analogies: mirror analogy and ocean analogy. What is the function of each? Why do we use one, and why do we use the other? What is the purpose of each? 

**Full Answer:**  
- Ocean and cup analogy: to be used for explaining the creation of mind in saincara and its association with an actual portion of the ocean of consciousness when unit mind emerges from stone at 6:00. The water within the cup is real water, and so is the jiivatma a real portion of purusa which has been linked with the unit mind.

- Mirror and flower analogy: for showing how Purusa is reflected in the mahattattva of unit mind, and that depending on how clean the mirror is, to that extent the unit mind will be aware of its link with purusa. This is also used together with the puddle of water example to show that the more developed a mind is, the better the reflection i.e. the more aware it becomes of the existence of an Infinite Entity and its own desire to become one with it and organize its entire existence around this effort.
