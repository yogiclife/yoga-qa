**Original Question:**  
Use of concepts of transitive and intransitive verb in explanation of mahattattva and ahamtattva.

**Full Answer:**  
Just want to make sure it is clear that the "I-feeling" of mahattattva that I had explained to Anita ji saying the "I feel" is intransitive whereas the "I know" is transitive and is expressed by ahamtattva-- it is not that the intransitive "I feel" always refers to mahattattva. Mahattattva only experiences the intransitive bhava of existence itself, nothing beyond that. You used the the examples "I feel good", "I feel light" -- but these two sentences could only be expressed by ahamtattva, not by mahattattva. The bhavas "good" and "light" go beyond the basic "I-feeling", which is all that is experienced in the mahattattva.
