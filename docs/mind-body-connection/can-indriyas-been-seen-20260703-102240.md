**Original Question:**  
Can we actually see the indriyas in the brain on autopsy? I was not aware of this. What are those indriyas called in Modern Science. 

**Full Answer:**  
So the brain is visible, and modern science has been able to find out what each part of the brain does. Although the optical cortex where visual processing is done is of course not labeled as such, but it is certainly visible to the naked eye; a microscope is also not needed. One can point to it with one's finger on autopsy.
For example, if I type into Google: "In what part of the brain is hearing processed?"

I get the reply:
============
Hearing is primarily processed in the temporal lobe, specifically in the auditory cortex. 
Here's a more detailed explanation:
Auditory Cortex:
This region, located in the temporal lobe, is responsible for interpreting and understanding sound signals sent from the auditory nerve. 
Primary Auditory Cortex:
This is the initial area within the auditory cortex that receives and processes basic auditory information, including pitch, tone, and frequency. 
Location:
The primary auditory cortex is located in the superior temporal gyrus of the temporal lobe and extends into the lateral sulcus and the transverse temporal gyri (also called Heschl's gyri). 
============

And so it is for each indriya. In yoga it is called the indriya: caksurindriya, shravaNendriya, etc. In modern science it is called the visual cortex, auditory cortex, etc.
