**Original Question:**  
In the realm of rupa, rasa, ganda, sparsha and shabda, how ahamtattva and chitta of the mind are involved is very clear. One thing is not clear as to how the mind is involved when karmendriyas are engaged in any action.   Let's say I am going from place A to place B, how is the citta and Ahamatattva involved in this action?

**Full Answer:**  
So in order to take any action, the mind first has to conceive of that action; it has to decide that it wants to take a certain action. The ahamtattva takes that action and conceives of it, gets a picture of it, on the citta (this can be and is often very brief), which then sends the message to the brain which in turn activates the roots of the efferent nerves in the spinal cord which send the message via the efferent peripheral nerves to the related muscles in the hand/feet/face etc so that they will begin the action.
