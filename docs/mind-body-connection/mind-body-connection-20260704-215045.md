**Original Question:**  
 If the mind is the one behind the indriyas to manifest into action/work, then many a times, human beings do things absent mindedly,  for ex. when giving directions  to someone driving, I say LEFT and do a  RIGHT side gesture with my hand. The gesture, is the correct one,always. So what was the mind doing and how and why it's intention misread by one of the indriyas?

Does it give a clue that  the mind- body  connection needs to be stronger??



**Full Answer:**  
So there are various levels of reflex arcs between the karmendriyas and the psychic apparatus which guides them. For example, if a person touches a hot pot, their hand will jump away from the pot before the mind realizes even what has happened. This is the design of the system, that if something dangerous is perceived, it reflexes back very quickly from a lower center of the brain rather than reaching up to the conscious mind for weighing the pros and cons. Only after pulling one's hand back does one realize, "oh yes, that was very hot, I hadn't known". And similarly, there are various levels of function ranging from the reflex arc just described, up through various levels of "habituated activity". So, for example, one a person is walking they do not need to consciously decide each time to move their right food forward, and then their left foot. If it had to be consciously decided, the mind would be constantly engaged in low-level decisions which would delay those basic activities, and would also not give the mind any time or opportunity for thinking about other things. So, because of the facility of habituated action, one can walk and think about various subjects and the same time-- the mind does not need to remain engaged in the act of walking. So in the example you gave, the hand engaged in a higher level of habitual level of activity (the mind may not have consciously thought, "Let me lift my hand and gesture to the right"). It happened at a level of the brain higher than the reflex arc of touching a hot pot, but lower than the conscious mind. And regarding the saying "left", and gesturing the right, this entire brain-mind apparatus is very sophisticated, but it is not infallible. The human mind makes mistakes. And it also needn't be that the verbalized expression would be incorrect and the hand gesture correct, it could have happened in the opposite way also, with the hand gesture incorrect. So in answer to your question, the mind-brain apparatus is definitely working and guiding the indriyas at every step. There are different levels of guidance, but that guidance is there. There is the low level guidance from basic brain centers which control walking, and there is the high level guidance which is involved when some is reading something, and the citta is taking the form of what is read on the page. But it all ultimately does come from the brain-mind center. If, for example, a person suffers a serious injury in which the spinal cord is damaged, then due to the connection between the brain-mind and the leg(s) or arm(s) involved, there will no longer be any sensations from that limb, and nor will the limb be able to move or do any work. This, even though the motor nerves, muscles and sensory nerves of that limb are still intact. No limb, no motor or sensory indriya can engage in activity without direction from above.
