**Original Question:**  
Upavasa Experiences

**Full Answer:**  
Great to hear everyone's experiences with upavasa.

- The first sister's experience was excellent. She did complete nirjalopavasa and also put in an excellent effort with fast breaking prakriya. Just will be good to let her know that she should continue taking salt water until what comes into the toilet has no color. The only reason she didn't reach to full cleaning in this first experience is that she stopped taking salt water-- she needs to keep drinking more salt water until cleaning is complete.

- The one senior brother has an obsession with nuts, and that too roasted (and salted?) nuts. Nuts should never be used as a way to fill the stomach, which is what he is doing. He should use fruits and veggies to fill the stomach. And if he wishes to take a few nuts, it should be just a few, and they need to be raw, not roasted. 

- Several people started the nirjalopavasa but did not complete it, either taking water during the day and not doing the cleaning prakriya the next morning, or taking food during the upavasa day itself, or not even trying at all due to fear. These people need reassurance and support that they can all do it. So give them plenty of encouragement and support, otherwise they will abandon the idea and never try it for the rest of their lives. One sister was telling about the "fasting" she does during navaratri-- that is not upavasa. Do encourage her to do nirjalopavasa upavasa with us on ekadashi, and she will get a new experience. 

- You explained to them so nicely about the benefits of upavasa and how it works in terms of both the spiritual and the physical/health benefits.
