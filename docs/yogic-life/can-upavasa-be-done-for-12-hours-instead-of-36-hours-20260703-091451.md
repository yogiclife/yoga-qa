**Original Question:**  
For those people who do not want to do 36 hours upavasa, can they do 12 hours upavasa and follow the cleaning process?

**Full Answer:**  
For those who do not feel able to do the nirjala upavasa for any reason but wish to try, I encourage them to do the 36-hour upavasa and take water until noon on the day of upavasa. Or if they want to take some food that day they may take some fruit up until noon. Then they can do the cleaning process the next morning. So the timing should be 36 hours. And then then next time they will have more confidence, and in this way gradually they can remove the fruit they were taking in the morning. 
I do not recommend a 12 hour period though, no. They have not done anything by this, it does not even provide time for the food from the prior night to be fully digested and to then enter a proper mode of upavasa. The time period should be maintained.
