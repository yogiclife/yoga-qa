**Full Answer:**  
I hope all are incorporating the yoga nisth jiivan into your daily lives. Over the course of one week at the shibiram you got a deep, blissful experience of what it feels like to do yoga sadhana three times a day, and yogasanas twice per day. And to eat a WFPB no SOS diet and run daily. These are the central elements of the yogic life. By following them the mind remains blissful 24 hours a day, and the body remains healthy and light. One should have full shaoca effortlessly on awakening, no need to drink water; and then again 2-3 times later in the day there will be complete shaoca, with the turnaround time between food and shaoca extremely low. The next item in the yogic life is nirjalopavasa, which we discussed but did not do during our week together. So nirjalopavasa you will get to try at home, and we look forward to hearing off your experience. No food or water for 36 hours, followed by shaoca prakriya the next morning. If you have not tried it yet, please do so the next ekadashii (Wed Jan 14 in India) and let us know your experience!
