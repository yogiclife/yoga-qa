**Original Question:**  
The question of sitting or standing while drinking water to break one's fast came up in X's session. 

**Full Answer:**  
It is true that while eating, drinking, and urinating, the general rule is that all three should be done while sitting. However, there is an exception to this, and I am sharing my own experience.

For years, while breaking a fast, I used to sit down to drink water, and in between when I wasn't drinking, I would stand up, walk around here and there. But maybe about three years ago, I also stood up while drinking water just to see what would happen. And I experienced that the water moves through the digestive tract much faster. Previously it used to take two hours, but now it takes an hour and a half—finished within 90 minutes. And the reason for this is standing the entire time. Nowadays, it takes 15 minutes for one liter, and after drinking three liters—meaning within 45 minutes—bowel movements begin.

The problem is that if we sit and drink water, there is a tendency to remain seated, and as long as we are sitting, the water moves very slowly through the digestive tract. If you stand the entire time, and walk around when you aren't drinking, it happens very quickly and comfortably. And even if someone is in no hurry, people will still find comfort because it happens quickly and without any strain. See for yourself next time if doing this makes a difference or not.
