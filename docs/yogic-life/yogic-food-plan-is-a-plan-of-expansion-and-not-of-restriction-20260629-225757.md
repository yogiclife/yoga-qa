**Original Question:**  
We teach what is the best food to eat. 

**Full Answer:**  
First, "restriction" is not the proper term. We are teaching what is the best food to eat. This is not at all a plan of restriction, but rather one of expansion. 

Next: You understand well what the scale of excellence is all about. We cannot tell students that getting 50% on the exam is sufficient. I do not support compromise in any aspect of the yogic life. From day one, people are to be taught what is the best approach in each aspect of human life. If they want to move in that direction gradually rather than quickly, that is their option. But telling them at the beginning that xyz is the goal when it is not the goal, that is not something we can support. 

I prefer to guide people by teaching what is the best thing and why--in the sphere of food and nutrition as well as in all spheres. How they choose to get there is their option, but we must teach them what the goal is. If people prefer to take ghee, curd, paneer, that is their option. But I will never say it is fine to do that. People coming to our sessions need to hear the truth. 

You as well as all our teachers are doing a very good job. 

Just see, we have a very strong team of sadhakas following the path of dharma-- Sarvajina ji, Onkar ji, Nitya ji, Vidhatrii ji, you, Ashok ji, Suchitra ji, Istadev ji, and all of you. You all didn't reach where you have reached by my giving a compromised approach. What I understand to be the best, that I give. If a student doesn't want the best, that is their option. But our duty is to provide the best. And again, "restriction" is not a good word to describe what we teach. We teach the path of dharma. And we teach following it strictly, surely. It is a path of positive action, one of adopting the best.

Now, teaching the path of dharma doesn't mean that if a person is not ready to follow 100% we won't welcome them. All are welcome. And indeed, all of you have a very supportive, encouraging, and welcoming environment in your classes. This is the right way to do it. Tell people the truth from the beginning, and support them as they strive to get there. Some will move slowly, some will move quickly-- we will support all. But if someone says, "Don't tell me the best path, tell me an easy path"-- that we cannot support. People will be taught the truth, the best-- from the beginning. 

Remember: our students have been members of a compromised path for their whole lives, and they are used to that. It is very comfortable for them. No one ever says anything to them about what to eat, and they like that. They hear high-sounding words in their houses of worship, and they don't have to do anything. No need to implement anything. So now they are hearing that they need to do vyapaka shaocha, they need to do nirjalopavasa, they need to eat a healthy sattvika diet-- and they prefer to remain asleep. The path of dharma is not for cowards. If they want to remain asleep, they will need to seek a bed elsewhere. That we will not give them. Arise, awake! Move from kali to dvapara, dvapara to treta, and treta to satya. We are teaching satya yuga. Those who are sincere and interested in living in satya yuga will be deeply moved and inspired by what we teach, and they will be eager to move ahead in every way.
