**Full Answer:**  
Salt ingestion is considered harmful to bone health primarily because it increases the amount of calcium excreted in the urine. This process can lead to bone thinning and a higher risk of developing osteoporosis.

Key ways salt ingestion affects bone health:

Increased Calcium Loss: High sodium intake triggers the kidneys to flush out sodium, which simultaneously causes calcium to be lost in the urine.

Bone Resorption: To maintain stable calcium levels in the blood, the body may draw calcium from the bones, a process called bone resorption, which can weaken bone structure over time.

Impact on Specific Groups:

Postmenopausal Women: Due to lower estrogen levels, this group is particularly susceptible to bone loss linked to high salt intake.

Adolescents: Excessive salt during youth may prevent reaching peak bone mass, increasing fracture risk later in life.

Indirect Effects: High salt diets are a major cause of high blood pressure, which further accelerates the rate of calcium loss from bones.
