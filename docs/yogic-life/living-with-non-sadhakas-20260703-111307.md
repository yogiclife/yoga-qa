**Original Question:**  
How to manage life with those who are not interested in yoga-nisth-jiivanam?

**Full Answer:**  
Guidelines for sadhakas living with non-sadhakas

- If one is a youth and not yet married, then it is a totally different subject as there is no permanent worldly commitment involved. If one has tried and things are not working out in a conducive manner, then one can simply move to a more conducive environment. People in this stage of life should be very, very careful about whether and/or whom to marry.

- For those who are already married and begin the yogic life thereafter, there is a whole continuum for approach. It should be made clear that our intention is not at all to break up families; rather, we would like to see families remain together and have all engage in the yogic life to the maximum possible extent. Indeed even if a husband or wife has no interest, the goal is to make it work staying together. That is not to say that separation or divorce should never be an option, but it is a last option that generally would require extreme and intolerable circumstances. And the decision-making tree for this also depends heavily on whether there are young children. 

Here are further recommendations for efforts to make the marriage work when one person is involved in yoga-nisth-jiivanam and the other is not yet interested:

- Try to get them to engage in whatever aspects they like: food, exercise, asanas, etc. See what they like.
- Three important points:
1. Personally be strict in all aspects of sadhaka life regardless of the situation. 
2. Try to have good company, i.e. satsang, with others so as to maintain a proper environment for forward movement.
3. Take help from others to convince your family members. 

- The very bad situation is when a sadhaka is dependent financially on a non-sadhaka family member. In such a situation, further considerations may be required. 

- Cook for yourself sentient food, do not depend on others to cook special food for you when they are disinterested in that food. 

- If there is no place in your home with a sentient environment to do sadhana then best is to go outside in the park or anywhere e.g. under the tree if it is not raining.

The above points are not intended to be all-inclusive of the various possible scenarios, and each situation needs to be considered separately.
