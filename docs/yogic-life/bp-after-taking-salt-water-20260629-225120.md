**Original Question:**  
BP after taking salt water

**Full Answer:**  
Firstly, regardless of what lifestyle a person may have, a single high BP reading cannot be depended on. One needs to check it several times, preferably over the course of a few days, to really know how the BP is, whether it is high or not. 

- Practicing nirjalopavasa with shaoca prakriya should if anything lower one's blood pressure, not raise it. There is cleansing going on, not only of the digestive tract but of the entire body-- the tissues, the blood -- everything.

It is not that the salt-water one uses in shaoca prakriya would cause an increase in blood pressure. After all, most of it remains in the GI tract and is excreted. And for a person following a WFPB no SOS diet, taking a bit of salt 2 to 4 times a month is a negligible amount indeed.
