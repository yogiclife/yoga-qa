**Original Question:**  
Clarification from Acharya ji

**Full Answer:**  
Regarding people's upavasa experience reports at the beginning of the session, two points--

1. One sister reported having not taken any food all day, but then the next morning her family "insisted she have breakfast with them", so she could not do the shaoca prakriya. She reported this as being something in which she had no option. But of course, she did have an option. She could have been polite but firm with her family and gone ahead with the shaoca prakriya. 

Same thing with one brother who reported that during the upavasa day his mouth became extremely dry and so he was "forced to take water" multiple times during the day, "had no option". 

And so these are not examples of situations in which one does not have an option. They describe the scenario as though it was one of being "forced" to do something. He was "forced" to drink water. So they need to see these situations in a different way, and then they will find that indeed they do have an option. Perhaps you can explain this to them in a positive, psychologically supportive way so they can learn how to face challenges on the path by opting for, choosing the dharmic approach. 

2. The sister who successfully did nirjalopavasa and also successfully did a great job! Just one thing I thought to mention, she reported that for several hours she was experiencing "irritation" in her bowels, by which she meant she needed to use the bathroom several more times. That is not irritation, nor is it a problem. It is a natural part of the shoaca prakriya to need to use the restroom multiple times, and that is a good thing. It is how the cleaning happens.
