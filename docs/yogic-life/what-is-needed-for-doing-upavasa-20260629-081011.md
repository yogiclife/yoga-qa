**Full Answer:**  
Two points-- 

1. In preparation for nirjalopavasa, you have discussed and explained well the need to take firm mental determination the day prior. And really, that is what is needed. And that is all that is needed. Due to lack of this point only, people do not get success.

This is just to say that the following two points are not needed:

- Drink plenty of water for the two days before nirjalopavasa. I have stopped drinking water entirely-- not only prior to upavasa but on all days. Raw fruits and vegetables have plenty of water in them and that water is of a far higher quality than what people drink from the tap or from some plastic bottle. So this is just by way of explaining that special preparation with drinking even more water than usual, is not needed. The body will just urinate out any excess water anyway, and the body has plenty of water in it for spending one day in nirjalopavasa.

- Eat plenty of carbohydrates the evening before. Not at all needed. Hearing this people will think they need to eat plenty of rice and potatoes the night before. I eat the same raw fruits and vegetables the night before nirjalopavasa as I eat every day. No need for eating heavy foods the night before nirjalopavasa. All that really does is make the breaking process more difficult because it has to clear those heavy foods. 

Let me also say that if these two points give people more confidence that they can do the nirjalopavasa and helps them in their mental determination, then it is fine to say this to them. I do not have any strong objection to them. Just I am saying that physiologically it is not needed.
